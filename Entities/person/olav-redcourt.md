## Biography

Olav Redcourt is a celebrated author known for his swashbuckling historical romance series featuring the Corsair King. In July 3304, he began an interstellar book tour for his fourteenth novel, "Corsair King and the Storm of Desire," visiting over fifty destinations throughout the core systems. During the tour, he made increasingly erratic comments to reporters, including "bounced across space like a damn human pinball" and "nothing but meat for the sausage factory."

Redcourt abandoned the tour in August 3304 and was later reported missing, sparking concern among fans and his publisher, Bonespire Publishing. He was found alive in November 3304 at a remote writers' retreat on the Rhea Archipelago, where he was working on a new science fiction novel. His agent, Margot Hist, explained that she had been unaware of the concern because she was on holiday and there were no communication facilities at Skyglow Havens. Redcourt's pivot to science fiction disappointed fans of the Corsair King series but highlighted the pressures facing commercial authors in the galactic publishing industry.
