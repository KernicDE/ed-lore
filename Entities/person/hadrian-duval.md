## Biography

Hadrian Duval is the grandson of Emperor Hengist Duval and a claimant to the Imperial throne through his father, the illegitimate Prince Harold Duval. Leader of the isolationist Nova Imperium movement, Hadrian challenged Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval's authority from his stronghold in the Paresa system, advocating a return to traditional Imperial values and withdrawal from galactic entanglements. His execution of Imperator Kaeso Mordanticus on the Senate floor in 3305 marked the brutal suppression of his movement — though Hadrian himself survived.

After the Nova Imperium's military defeat and the purge of its supporters, Hadrian accepted a pardon from Arissa and was granted the title of Lord, effectively neutralising him as a political threat while preserving the Duval bloodline. His subsequent marriage to Lady Astrid Minerva-Duval and the birth of their son Hector in 3307 transformed Hadrian from rebel to family man, though questions about his true ambitions and lingering support among isolationist sympathisers continue to shadow his public rehabilitation.
