## Biography

Harlow Nassry is a technology journalist for the Federal Times who has reported on major developments in computing, cybercrime and corporate innovation. In August 3304, he highlighted two new personal computer designs challenging the Sirius and Achilles Corporation monopoly: Supratech's Torc, a flexible wrist or neck ring using holographic projectors, and Herculean Machines' Duradrive, a durable tablet for rough handling.

Nassry also published an article in October 3304 about the Collective, a mysterious group of anti-authority hacktivists responsible for digital crimes including the theft of a billion credits from Zachary Rackham. His reporting on the Collective revealed that the group believed all information should be publicly available, and that their anarchist ideology made them nearly impossible to investigate due to their lack of leaders or spokespersons.
