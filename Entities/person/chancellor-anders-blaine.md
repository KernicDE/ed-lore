# Chancellor Anders Blaine

## Role
Chancellor of the Empire and senior statesman. Acts as regent and chief advisor during Emperor Hengist Duval's declining health.

## Character
Blaine is portrayed as overwhelmed and politically weakened. His inability to challenge Senator Torval's legally questionable slave import operations suggests either incompetence, complicity, or a calculated decision to avoid confrontation during a sensitive period. Sources close to him dismiss Torval's actions as "pathetic attempts" that he is "too busy" to address.

## Timeline of Significance
- **3301-03-05:** Senate refuses to address Torval's unregulated slave imports; Blaine's weakness is noted by political commentators

## Key Relationships
- **Emperor Hengist Duval** — Serves as chancellor under the ailing Emperor
- **Senator Zemina Torval** — Exploits his weakness to expand her operations

## Modern Status (3312)
Blaine's waning authority in early 3301 is symptomatic of the broader institutional decay preceding the Emperor's death. His failure to maintain Senate discipline contributes to the power vacuum that enables the succession crisis. Whether he is a victim of circumstance or an enabler of chaos remains debated by historians.
