## Biography

Gudrun Vestergaard is the royal correspondent for the Imperial Herald who has reported on major developments in Imperial politics and the Duval family. In January 3309, she commented on Princess Aisling Duval's public appeal to Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval to join the Alliance and Federation in reforming Aegis, noting that the move could cause friction between Aisling and her cousin Hadrian Duval.

Vestergaard observed that while both Aisling and Hadrian had previously enjoyed a close relationship — with Aisling even chosen as honour-mother to Hadrian's baby son Hector — their views on Imperial foreign policy "could not be more different." Her reporting on the Imperial family has provided insight into the tensions between reformist and isolationist factions within the Empire, particularly during the critical period when the Thargoid invasion forced a re-evaluation of Imperial non-intervention policies.
