## Biography

### Early Career and Federal Research Programme

Professor Ishmael Palin began his career as a member of the Federation's Homeland Scientific Council. In September 3301, he was appointed to lead a Federal research programme studying the strange quasi-organic objects discovered in systems including Tanmark and HIP 102128 — artefacts dubbed "anomalous extraterrestrial objects" (AEOs) or "unknown artefacts" by the galactic community.

The programme, based at Fort Harrison in the Arcturus system, was abruptly terminated on 20 September 3301 after only one week. Governor Aoishe Quaid ordered Palin to cease his research immediately, a decision that sources at Fort Harrison believed was made under external pressure rather than public safety concerns. The objects were capable of broadcasting location data across vast distances and damaging nearby machinery — widely considered the scientific find of the decade.

Palin initially declined to comment on the termination. When the Canonn Interstellar Research Group invited him to join their independent scientific body, he declined, stating: "I admire the enthusiasm of the Canonn Interstellar Research Group, but I sense that our respective philosophies concerning the anomalous extraterrestrial objects are somewhat at odds with each other."

### The Palin Institute and Independent Research

On 26 October 3301, Palin announced his defiant return to research, establishing the Palin Institute as an independent organisation at Christian Dock in the Nganji system. He declared his intention to share findings with the galactic community "free from interference." The decision was not without risk — on 2 November 3301, armed intruders carrying illegal weapons and incendiary explosives were apprehended at Christian Dock while attempting to break into Palin's laboratory.

In December 3301, Palin appealed to the galactic community for evacuation shelters to establish a new research outpost on Maia B1BA in the Pleiades Nebula, close to Merope where non-human structures had been discovered. The appeal was enthusiastically received, and the new base became operational within weeks. By January 3302, Palin had relocated to Obsidian Orbital in the Maia system and issued a call for pilots to deliver meta-alloys — materials produced by the newly discovered alien "barnacles" — believing they might counter the technological plague affecting starports across the galaxy.

### Kidnapping and Aftermath

On 9 February 3302, Palin's ship went missing en route to Maia B1BA, shortly after departing Obsidian Orbital. A search was launched by Maia system authorities, but no trace of the vessel was found. Nearly a month later, on 5 March 3302, Palin and his research team were discovered among escape pods recovered from the Blue Hand Gang, a criminal syndicate operating in the Orulas system. Military veteran Rose Trebek speculated the gang had purchased the pods without knowing their contents.

After recovering at Wilkes Orbital, Palin described his ordeal in disturbing detail. Black-clad assailants had destroyed his escort ships, boarded his vessel, and interrogated him for days about his Unknown Artefact research. "They were convinced I was withholding something," he stated. When asked who was responsible, Palin said: "I do not know the name of their organisation, but I know what they represent: control, oppression, and the obviation of civil liberties. Such organisations have existed for millennia — the banner under which they march is immaterial."

### Retirement and Continued Research

The kidnapping left Palin deeply shaken. On 15 April 3302, he announced his retirement from the Palin Institute, relinquishing directorship to his closest ally, Professor Cora Shaw. He stated: "There are dark forces at work in the galactic community — forces that exist in opposition to the ideals I hold most dear — and I lack the strength to continue fighting them."

Palin did not abandon research entirely, however. He established a personal research centre on Maia A 3 a, continuing his work on Unknown Artefacts and meta-alloys in a more inconspicuous fashion. Over the following years, he also developed a reputation as an engineer specialising in thrusters and frame shift drives, offering modifications to independent pilots who could prove their worth.

### Engineering Career and Relocation

On 18 July 3305, a Thargoid offensive in the Maia system forced Palin and his staff to evacuate from the Palin Research Centre. With assistance from the megaship Carson's Spring, they relocated to Abel Laboratory on Arque 4 E, where Palin resumed both his engineering services and his meta-alloy studies. Aegis Research confirmed the relocation through Professor Alba Tesreau.

Palin's engineering services require pilots to have travelled at least 5,000 light years from their career start system and to provide 25 units of sensor fragments for access. He specialises in Grade 5 thruster modifications (clean, dirty, and strengthened tuning) and Grade 3 frame shift drive improvements (faster boot sequence, increased range, and shielded variants). His former apprentice, Chloe Sedesi, later established her own engineering workshop at Cinder Dock in the Shenve system within the Witch Head Nebula.

### Thargoid War Contributions

Palin remained an active researcher throughout the Thargoid conflict. In August 3308, he collaborated with Ram Tah on a study of "unclassified relics" — Guardian artefacts that had undergone molecular realignment after interfacing with Thargoid surface sites, resulting in objects that emitted both Guardian and Thargoid energy signatures. This challenged the assumption that the two technologies were fundamentally incompatible.

In November 3309, Palin announced a breakthrough in the study of Thargoid spire sites. By analysing compounds from spire refineries and comparing them with Titan hull fragments, his team verified genetic commonalities and synthesised a biological contaminant designed to disrupt spire processing chains. He theorised that the spires functioned as supply chains sustaining Thargoid motherships, and that their logistical strain might indicate dwindling vital enzymes or fuels after months of deployment away from Thargoid home worlds.

Palin also contributed to the Guardian nanite torpedo project in early 3310, working with Aegis to develop weapons capable of penetrating Thargoid Titan defences. His decades of accumulated expertise on Thargoid biology and technology made him one of the most consulted scientists in the anti-xeno effort.

### Philosophy and Legacy

Throughout his career, Palin has maintained a consistent commitment to open scientific inquiry in the face of forces seeking to suppress it. From his initial refusal to speculate on the origin of Unknown Artefacts — "It would be imprudent of me to speculate on the objects' origin or purpose" — to his later warnings about "dark forces" opposing civil liberties, Palin has embodied the tension between scientific curiosity and political power in the 34th century.

His work spans the full arc of human-Thargoid contact: from the first uncertain studies of barnacles and meta-alloys in the Pleiades, through the kidnapping that nearly silenced him, to the cutting-edge xeno-weapons research of the 3310s. Few scientists have survived as many attempts to halt their work, or contributed as directly to humanity's defence against the Thargoids.
