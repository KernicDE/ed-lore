## Biography

Professor Alba Tesreau was the preeminent scientific voice in humanity's response to the Second Thargoid War. As head of the Achenar Research Council, she possessed unparalleled expertise in xenology — the study of alien life — and was the natural choice to lead Aegis's scientific division when the organisation was formed in August 3303.

Tesreau's approach to the Thargoid threat was methodical and collaborative. She established research outposts across the Pleiades Nebula, directed studies of Thargoid barnacles and meta-alloys, and oversaw the development of the first effective anti-xeno missiles and shutdown field neutralisers. Her preference for understanding the Thargoids rather than simply destroying them put her at odds with militarists in all three superpowers, but her scientific achievements were undeniable. Aegis's research into meta-alloys proved crucial for repairing stations damaged in Thargoid attacks, and her team's work on Guardian technology integration provided pilots with powerful new tools against the alien threat.

The Alexandria disaster shattered both Aegis and Tesreau's career. In 3307, the Aegis megaship Alexandria vanished with 4,000 crew and a cargo of Guardian artefacts. The superpowers ordered an independent inquiry, and the Baumann Report — completed in January 3308 — recommended Aegis's dissolution. Tesreau was forced to resign in August 3307, along with Director Yazhu Xing and Dr. Mia Valencourt. The report criticised her for focusing on Thargoid communication research rather than developing new generations of AX weaponry, and for organisational complacency.

Tesreau refused to accept the narrative that Aegis was solely responsible for the Alexandria's loss. Operating independently, she received a tip about drifting objects sighted outside the Core Systems and organised a search by independent pilots. In January 3308, the Alexandria's wreckage was located in Wregoe TC-X b29-0. Captain Morales's logs revealed that the ship had been sabotaged and raided by Azimuth Biochemicals operatives working for "The Witch" — not destroyed by Thargoids as officially claimed. This discovery vindicated Tesreau and exposed a conspiracy that the superpowers had been unwilling to investigate.

Following Aegis's dissolution, Tesreau became one of Salvation's most determined adversaries. In May 3308, she revealed the location of INRA base Iraxon Lane, where logs confirmed that Salvation was Dr. Caleb Wycherley, former vice-president of Azimuth Biochemicals and a contributor to the mycoid bioweapon. She exposed his role in the Alexandria sabotage and his manipulation of the superpowers through Project Seraph. These revelations were dismissed by Salvation's supporters and ignored by politicians who had become dependent on his superweapons, but Tesreau's investigative work provided the definitive account of Salvation's true nature.

Tesreau's legacy is that of a scientist who refused to abandon principle even when it cost her position, reputation, and institutional support. Her research saved countless lives during the Thargoid conflicts, and her investigative work exposed one of the most dangerous figures in modern galactic history.
