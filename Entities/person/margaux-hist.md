Margaux Hist worked as a literary agent affiliated with Bonespire Publishing during the mid-3300s, representing authors operating within the niche science-fiction genre. Her professional responsibilities included managing client relationships, negotiating publishing contracts, and guiding the public presentation of her authors' work. In 3304, she found herself managing the increasingly erratic behaviour of Redcourt, an author whose cryptic public comments about being "bounced across space" and references to "meat for the sausage factory" raised concerns among readers and critics alike.

Hist's approach to these controversies reflected the pragmatism required of publishing professionals navigating unconventional talent. She dismissed Redcourt's more alarming statements as "character development thinking," while privately expressing hope that the book's sales would generate sufficient revenue to cover mounting legal bills. Her experience illustrated the challenges facing agents who represented artists whose creative impulses did not always align with conventional commercial or legal expectations.
