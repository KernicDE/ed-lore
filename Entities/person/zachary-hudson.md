## Biography

Zachary Hudson was born in 3218 and built his political career in the Federation's Republican Party, becoming a prominent Congressman and ultimately Shadow President during Jasmina Halsey's Liberal administration. He was Halsey's most vocal critic, attacking her military interventions and what he characterised as government overreach. When Halsey and Vice President Ethan Naylor disappeared aboard Starship One on 26 May 3301, Secretary of State Felicia Winters briefly became Acting President. On 2 June 3301, Congress passed a Vote of No Confidence against the absent Halsey and installed Hudson as President, with Brad Mitchell as his Vice President.

Hudson's presidency was defined by militarism and corporate alignment. He increased military spending, supported expanded naval training facilities, and encouraged public respect for the armed forces — despite never having served himself. He was a proponent of the Close Quarters Combat championship as a naval recruitment tool. His famous declaration on Thargoid contact in June 3301 encapsulated his philosophy: "If the Thargoids want a fight, it's a fight they'll get." He regularly vacationed on frontier worlds for big-game hunting, cultivating an image of rugged toughness.

The defining scandal of Hudson's presidency emerged years after his rise. In 3307, Federal High Court trials revealed that Admiral Lucas Vincent — Hudson's close friend and ally — had conspired with Core Dynamics CEO Jupiter Rochester to destroy Starship One in 3301, assassinating Halsey to install Hudson in power and ensure lucrative military contracts for Core Dynamics. Subsequent investigations found no evidence that Hudson personally knew of or participated in the plot, but the association severely damaged his credibility. The scandal also ensnared his administration's surveillance programmes, particularly the Proactive Detection Bureau (PDB), which monitored private communications across Federal space.

In 3305, Hudson faced the mandatory Vote of No Confidence required after a president's first four years. He survived by a narrow margin, allowing him to complete his extended term. By 3309, Liberal candidate Felicia Winters had gained sufficient support to defeat Hudson's chosen successor, Jerome Archer, in the Federal election. Hudson left office on 31 October 3310, defeated but unrepentant, having pushed through last-minute legislation including corporate tax rebates and a merger of the PDB into Federal Navy Intelligence. His legacy is a Federation more militarised, more surveilled, and more corporatised than when he found it.
