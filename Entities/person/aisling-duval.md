## Biography

Aisling Duval was born to Prince Harold Duval and entertainer Imogen Gabrielini, who died of a narcotics-induced aneurysm shortly after her birth. When Emperor Hengist decreed Harold "not sound of mind" in December 3300, Aisling was excluded from the succession. She supported her half-sister Arissa Lavigny-Duval's claim rather than contest it.

Despite exclusion from the throne, Aisling built a powerful political following. In 3301, she founded Stop Slavery Stupid and challenged Senator Zemina Torval to a public emancipation contest, freeing over 1.1 million slaves. In 3302 she merged the charity into Unchain, broadening her reform platform. She survived an assassination attempt by Emperor's Dawn and has consistently condemned INRA's experiments on live Thargoids.

Aisling's personal life has generated significant attention, including a brief engagement to Federal Ambassador Jordan Rochester in 3303 — a cross-superpower romance that scandalised traditionalists before its cancellation. Critics dismiss her as naive, yet she has undeniably shifted the Imperial conversation on slavery and social justice, with popularity among common citizens unrivalled by any other Imperial Family member.
