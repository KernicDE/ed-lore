## Biography

Aisling Duval was born to Prince Harold Duval — the only legitimate son and heir of Emperor Hengist Duval — and Imogen Gabrielini, a popular entertainer known as "Eccentrica" who died of a narcotics-induced aneurysm shortly after Aisling's birth. In December 3300, Emperor Hengist decreed that Harold was "not sound of mind" and unfit to rule, removing both Harold and his descendants from the line of succession. This decree, combined with Aisling's illegitimate birth, eliminated her as a contender for the Imperial throne. When her half-sister Arissa Lavigny declared herself Hengist's illegitimate daughter in December 3300, Aisling supported Arissa's claim rather than contest it.

Despite her exclusion from the succession, Aisling built an extraordinary political following. In early 3301, she appeared on the reality stream show *Celebrity Pets* and delivered a passionate condemnation of Imperial slavery, declaring that the animals on the show and in Senator Zemina Torval's personal zoo were better treated than Imperial slaves. She founded the charity Stop Slavery Stupid, which announced temporary emancipation and rehabilitation programmes. In April 3301, she challenged Senator Torval to a public support contest: Torval's faction would buy unregulated slaves and convert them to Imperial slaves, while Aisling's would accept donations of Imperial slaves for emancipation. Though Torval won the contest, Aisling's side freed 1,149,756 slaves.

In 3302, Aisling merged Stop Slavery Stupid with another anti-slavery charity to form Unchain, broadening her reform platform. She survived an assassination attempt by Emperor's Dawn, a hardline insurgent group opposed to the Duval dynasty. Her security was subsequently overhauled. In 3303, she condemned the terrorist Salomé (Kahina Tijani Loren) as "a force of evil," and later criticised Aegis's handling of the Thargoid threat. She also spoke out against the INRA's historical experiments on live Thargoids, calling them sickening and suggesting the organisation may have fabricated the alien threat to justify its military programme.

Aisling's personal life has generated as much attention as her politics. She was briefly engaged to Federal Ambassador Jordan Rochester in 3303 — a cross-superpower romance that scandalised traditionalists in both the Empire and Federation before the wedding was cancelled. She has also reached out to her cousin Hadrian Augustus Duval, the exiled leader of Nova Imperium, establishing a non-aggression pact in 3305 and encouraging him to reconsider his isolationist policies.

Critics dismiss Aisling as naive, inexperienced, and overly conscious of her public "brand." Her defenders — and even some of her sharpest critics — suggest that her apparent vacuity is a carefully cultivated front, developed with advisors who exploit her image while she pursues genuine reform. Either way, she has undeniably shifted the Imperial conversation on slavery and social justice, and her popularity among common citizens remains unrivalled by any other member of the Imperial Family.
