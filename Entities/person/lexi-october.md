## Biography

Lexi October is a billionaire investor and corporate executive who has built her career around acquiring and restructuring distressed technology firms. In 3305, she rescued Supratech from bankruptcy after the company's Torc personal computer design proved commercially impractical, organising a commodity drive to restart production. Her intervention demonstrated a pattern of using wealth and community resource campaigns to acquire engineering talent and intellectual property from failing competitors.

October's most significant — and controversial — corporate holding is the October Consortium's control of the HIP 87621 system, which she acquired through permit rights over a decade ago. In the 3310s, she defended her organisation's secrecy regarding anomalous exobiological readings in the system, dismissing data inconsistencies as "spoofing" and deflecting scrutiny by questioning why rival billionaire Zachary Rackham had failed to share exploration data. Her opaque management of HIP 87621 made her a central figure in one of the decade's most contentious scientific access disputes.
