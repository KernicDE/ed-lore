## Biography

Jasmina Halsey was elected President of the Federation in 3300 as a Liberal, promising social reform and expanded government services. Her presidency quickly unravelled. In December 3300 she criminalised the narcotic Onionhead and launched a military campaign against growers in the Kappa Fornacis system; the operation went awry when Federal forces firebombed civilian farms, causing significant collateral damage. Her approval rating began a steep decline.

The defining disaster of Halsey's presidency was the War for Lugh. In early 3301, an independent faction seized control of the Federal system of Lugh and declared independence. Halsey responded with military force, but the campaign proved poorly planned and executed. The Federation suffered an embarrassing defeat, and Halsey's reputation collapsed entirely when she ordered Federal pilots to attack an unarmed refugee convoy fleeing the system — reportedly believing that a rebel leader was aboard. Approximately 9,322 innocent Federal civilians died. Her approval rating plummeted to 26%, then to a historic low of 16% by April 3301.

On 26 May 3301, Starship One — Halsey's presidential vessel — disappeared during a goodwill tour of Federal frontier systems. Secretary of State Felicia Winters became Acting President, and on 2 June 3301 Congress passed a Vote of No Confidence that formally removed Halsey and installed Zachary Hudson. For months she was presumed dead.

In February 3302, President Hudson organised a public search for Starship One wreckage. Independent pilots recovered thousands of escape pods, and on 26 February 3302 Hudson announced that Halsey's pod had been found. She was revived from an induced coma in March 3302, but immediately sparked concern with her account of the disaster. She claimed to have encountered "the caretakers of the galaxy" — super-intelligent beings who communicated with her while she drifted in cryostasis, showing her "the infinities of the cosmos" and delivering a message of peace.

Halsey's "visions" were widely ridiculed and politically toxic. She expressed no interest in returning to the presidency and relocated to Alliance space, where by April 3304 she had become a peace activist and advisor to Prime Minister Edmund Mahon. Her critics accused her of treason and speculated that she had orchestrated her disappearance to defect with Federal intelligence; Halsey dismissed these claims, pointing to her humanitarian work and her condemnation of the Federal-Imperial cold war.

In 3307, Halsey returned to Federal territory to provide witness testimony at the trial of Fleet Admiral Lucas Vincent, who was convicted of conspiring to destroy Starship One. Following the trial, she joined the Federal Diplomatic Corps as an ambassador to the Alliance, formalising the role she had effectively been performing for years. Her transformation from discredited warmonger to respected peace advocate remains one of the most remarkable political journeys in Federal history.
