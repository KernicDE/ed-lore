## Biography

Aden Tanner was one of the most experienced naval commanders in the Federation before his appointment as Aegis's chief military liaison in 3303. As Chief of Federal Security, he had overseen counter-intelligence and internal defence operations across Federal space — experience that proved valuable when the Thargoid threat emerged and the superpowers were forced to cooperate.

Tanner's role in Aegis was primarily operational. He coordinated military responses to Thargoid incursions, directed the deployment of defence forces to threatened stations, and commanded the Aegis Defence megaship Musashi. While Professor Alba Tesreau focused on scientific research, Tanner handled the military reality of the conflict — organising evacuation operations, defending Pleiades outposts, and planning counter-offensives against Thargoid forces.

Tanner was among the first senior military figures to distrust Salvation. In September 3307, when Salvation claimed to have developed a superweapon capable of clearing entire systems of Thargoids, Tanner suspected that the weapon's "successful" demonstration in Cornsar had been staged — that Salvation had deliberately lured Thargoids into the system by stockpiling Guardian artefacts, using the civilian population as unwitting test subjects. He shared these concerns with Professor Tesreau and attempted to gather evidence through official channels.

When official channels failed, Tanner took drastic action. In November 3307, he commandeered the Musashi and launched an unauthorised attack on Hind Mine in the T Tauri system — the headquarters of Taurus Mining Ventures, Salvation's corporate front. His objective was to seize evidence of Salvation's manipulation and criminal activities. However, Tanner underestimated Salvation's support among independent pilots. Forces loyal to Salvation overwhelmed the Musashi, defeated Tanner's task force, and detained the Admiral.

Tanner was court-martialed for mutiny and unlawful military action. The trial, initially scheduled for early 3308, was suspended after Ram Tah — the renowned engineer and Guardian technology expert — provided evidence that Salvation's superweapons contained Guardian components derived from the Proteus site. This vindicated Tanner's suspicions but came too late to alter the political calculus: the superpowers had already committed to Salvation's approach, and Aegis's dissolution was imminent.

Tanner's career exemplifies the dilemma faced by honourable officers in dishonourable times. His military judgement was correct — Salvation was indeed a manipulator who sacrificed civilian lives and provoked Thargoid escalation — but his willingness to act on that judgement outside the chain of command destroyed his career and reputation. Whether history will remember him as a patriot or a mutineer remains debated.
