## Biography

Congressman Bai Zheng is a Federal legislator who emerged as one of the most vocal critics of the Proactive Detection Bureau's surveillance programme. In 3307, he famously remarked that the PDB was "incapable of locating the NMLA, but fantastic at finding enemies among our own people that didn't previously exist" — a line that became a rallying cry for civil liberties advocates.

Zheng's criticism came after an Advocacy report exposed that the FIA was using PDB-intercepted communications to target pro-independence individuals for arrest, reclassifying secessionist planning as criminal acts against the Federal Accord. While Vice President Jerome Archer defended the PDB for boosting arrest rates, Zheng's pithy condemnation captured the growing concern that emergency surveillance powers had been repurposed to suppress domestic political opposition rather than combat terrorism. His stance placed him among a minority of Federal congressmen willing to challenge the surveillance state from within the halls of power.
