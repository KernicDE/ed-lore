# Bai Zheng

Bai Zheng is a Federal Congressman known for his outspoken criticism of executive overreach. In July 3307, he delivered a memorable condemnation of the Proactive Detection Bureau after it was revealed that intercepted communications were being used to prevent factions from leaving the Federation. Zheng's remark that the PDB was "incapable of locating the NMLA, but fantastic at finding enemies among our own people that didn't previously exist" captured the frustration of civil liberties advocates who argued the surveillance network had been redirected from counter-terrorism to domestic political control. His statement highlighted growing dissent within Congress over Hudson's security policies.
