## Biography

Arissa Lavigny-Duval was born in 3243 during an affair between Emperor Hengist Duval and Florence Lavigny, a member of the prestigious Lavigny family. For decades she was raised as the legitimate daughter of Prince Aristide de Lavigny, becoming a senator in her own right and building a reputation for tackling corruption and championing honour in Imperial politics. Her primary platform — anti-corruption and the restoration of Imperial dignity — earned her considerable support among traditionalists and reformers alike.

The succession crisis of 3300–3301 transformed her life. In December 3300, Emperor Hengist decreed that his legitimate son Prince Harold Duval was "not sound of mind" and unfit to rule. On 20 December 3300, Arissa publicly declared that she was Hengist's biological daughter and changed her surname to Lavigny-Duval. Hengist endorsed her claim and announced his intention to marry Florence Lavigny, which would have legitimised Arissa and secured her succession. However, Hengist fell into a coma, and the wedding was repeatedly postponed.

On 5 August 3301, Hengist was assassinated on his wedding day by Brendan Paul Darius, a member of the hardline insurgent group Emperor's Dawn. The Emperor died at the age of 138. With the succession now urgent, the Imperial Senate deliberated for two months. Arissa faced competing claims from Princess Aisling Duval (Hengist's granddaughter through Harold) and Senator Denton Patreus, who had positioned himself as a strongman candidate. On 6 October 3301, the Senate elected Arissa by a clear majority. She was crowned on 18 October 3301, becoming the first woman to sit on the Imperial Throne — a milestone made possible by Hengist's earlier reform permitting female succession.

Arissa's reign immediately faced existential challenges. In 3303, she condemned the terrorist Salomé and supported military action against Thargoid incursions. The discovery of abandoned INRA bases in 3303 revealed the dark history of the First Thargoid War, complicating her government's position on xeno relations. The Marlinist refugee crisis of 3306–3307, triggered by the Neo-Marlinist Liberation Army's terrorist campaign, forced the Empire to confront republican dissent within its borders. Arissa approved the construction of Marlinist Colonies by Sirius Corporation as a solution, while also authorising crackdowns on NMLA sympathisers.

In January 3307, Arissa herself was abducted by the Lords of Restoration — a secretive group of Imperial senators — while en route to a state function. The kidnapping, which occurred alongside the assassinations of Prince Harold Duval and other Duval family members, was covered up with a body double. She was eventually rescued, but the incident exposed deep fractures within the Imperial power structure. Her subsequent reign has been characterised by efforts to rebuild central authority while managing the ongoing Thargoid threat and internal dissent from groups like Nova Imperium and the NMLA.

Arissa Lavigny-Duval's governance style combines her father's cautious conservatism with her own rigorous emphasis on honour and anti-corruption. She has maintained the Empire's traditional institutions while adapting to unprecedented external pressures, making her one of the most consequential — and embattled — rulers in Imperial history.
