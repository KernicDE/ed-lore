## Biography

Senator Zemina Torval is one of the most powerful and enduring figures in the Imperial Senate, representing the solid traditionalist wing of Imperial politics. Born into wealth, she nevertheless maintains a strong work ethic and has built her influence through a combination of senatorial authority, vast business holdings, and careful political alliances.

### Business Empire

Torval is famously the controlling shareholder of **Mastopolos Mining**, one of the largest mining conglomerates in Imperial space. She is also a major shareholder in the **East India Company**, an Imperial trade organisation. Her business interests extend into the narcotics trade through the East India Company's involvement with Onionhead farmers in Kappa Fornacis — a connection that drew the ire of Federal President Zachary Hudson in 3301.

### The Slavery Debate and Aisling Duval

Torval's most public ideological battle has been with Princess Aisling Duval over the institution of Imperial slavery. In March 3301, Aisling appeared on the talk show *A Fireside Chat With Serena* and called Torval "ancient, misguided, possibly evil," declaring that "slavery is stupid." Torval responded through action rather than words.

The two figures went head-to-head in what Imperial citizens dubbed the **"Great Slaver Showdown"** (April 3301). Aisling's Angels freed 1,149,756 slaves, while supporters of the Imperial Slavers Association — operating under Torval's patronage — "provided for" 2,387,567 non-citizens through their training facilities. Torval's side won decisively, and she authorised the sale of 5 million Imperial Slaves at a 20% discount at Lagerkvist Gateway in Synteini.

Torval's position on slavery is characteristically paradoxical: she is vocal in condemning those who fail to care for their slaves properly, while maintaining that slavery forms the foundation of the Empire. In March 3301, she opened a new ISA slave training facility aboard Lagerkvist Gateway featuring "skill implantation terminals and body sculpting shops."

### The Emperor's Assassination and Succession (3301)

Following the murder of Emperor Hengist Duval in August 3301, Torval moved carefully through the succession crisis. When Senator Denton Patreus faced public abuse over his connection to assassin Brendan Paul Darius, Torval was the first Senate heavyweight to defend him, calling the mob behaviour "near-barbarism" and stating that "anyone suggesting [Patreus's disloyalty] is a fool."

In late August 3301, Torval declared her support for Patreus's military campaign against Emperor's Dawn, the insurgent group claiming responsibility for the regicide. She instructed her followers to join the campaign, helping rehabilitate Patreus's political standing.

However, as the succession consolidated, Torval shifted her support to **Arissa Lavigny-Duval**. On 28 September 3301, she announced her backing for Arissa's claim to the throne — a move that, combined with Chancellor Blaine's support and Patreus's public endorsement, effectively sealed Arissa's path to becoming Empress.

### Federal Confrontations

Torval has been a consistent critic of Federal expansionism. In July 3302, she condemned the Federation's deployment of warships in the Merope system, accusing it of attempting to claim possession of barnacles and meta-alloys. She declared the Federal presence "unacceptable" and warned that it heralded further erosion of Empire-Federation relations.

### Personality and Methods

Political commentator Marcus Macmillan profiled Torval in August 3301 as a figure who "lacks the impetuousness of Patreus, but is quite willing to work with him to achieve her goals." She prefers diplomacy to force, but commands a substantial military fleet capable of handling most situations.

Torval's desire for power is framed not as mere personal ambition but as dynastic responsibility. She is regarded as the matriarch of a developing dynasty, focusing her energy on building a legacy for future generations. Family is very important to her, and in her younger days she enjoyed hunting — a pastime she has since limited to "her rivals in business and the Senate."
