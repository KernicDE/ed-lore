Arissa Lavigny-Duval ascended to the Imperial throne in 3301 following the death of her father, Emperor Hengist Duval, becoming the first woman to rule the Empire in centuries. Her reign was immediately tested by crises including the emergence of Emperor's Dawn, a republican movement that challenged the very foundation of Imperial governance. Throughout her rule, she pursued policies that combined traditional Imperial values with pragmatic adaptations to the changing galactic landscape, maintaining the Empire's position as one of the three dominant superpowers while navigating internal dissent and external threats.

Her documented appearances in GalNet records from 3302 through 3307 reflected both her ceremonial duties and her engagement with substantive policy matters, from military appointments to diplomatic receptions. The relative scarcity of direct quotations in the archive compared to other heads of state suggests either a preference for governing through intermediaries or the Empire's success in controlling the narrative around its monarch. Arissa's reign continued through the most turbulent period in recent Imperial history, including the Thargoid invasion and the NMLA terrorist campaign that would eventually test the Empire's resilience to its core.
