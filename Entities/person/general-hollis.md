General Hollis commanded security forces in the Betel system during the violent conflict between Betel Free and the Silver Creative Network in mid-3304. His position required him to maintain order amid factional warfare that had escalated from political disagreement to open combat, threatening civilian populations and infrastructure across the system. The general's public statements during this period revealed both his desire for peaceful resolution and the practical constraints limiting his forces' ability to intervene effectively.

Following the cessation of hostilities, Hollis expressed hope that the system would enjoy a prolonged period of peace, though his earlier admission that security forces lacked sufficient ships for effective intervention underscored the resource limitations facing local authorities. His experience in Betel illustrated the challenges confronting system security leaders when conflicts between well-equipped factions exceeded the capacity of official forces to control. Hollis's career demonstrated how military commanders in frontier systems often found themselves positioned between competing interests without adequate resources to enforce their preferred outcomes.
