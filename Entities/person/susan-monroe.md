## Biography

Susan Monroe was a Federal Security Service agent who served on Vice President Nigel Smeaton's protection detail. In 3301–3302, she became a key figure in the investigation into Smeaton's death when journalist Elaine Boyd of the Federal Times was accused of Monroe's murder. The Federal Times later revealed that Monroe was alive and part of Smeaton's security team at the time of his death, suggesting she had willingly provided Boyd with information about the case. Monroe's cooperation with investigative journalism helped expose potential corruption within the highest levels of Federal government, making her a significant figure in one of the Federation's most controversial political scandals.
