Oliver Gordon established himself as a chef of considerable talent, founding a restaurant under the name 'PanGalactic' that attracted attention across its local region for its distinctive culinary offerings. His choice of name, apparently inspired by the vast scope of human settlement rather than any specific corporate affiliation, nevertheless drew the ire of Pan Galactic Mining Corp, one of the galaxy's larger resource extraction companies. The corporation's legal team served Gordon with a writ demanding he change the restaurant's name, claiming trademark infringement on their established brand.

The ensuing legal battle proved financially devastating for Gordon, who lacked the resources to mount a sustained defence against a major corporate entity's legal apparatus. Within days of the initial filing, mounting legal costs forced him to close his restaurant entirely, destroying a venture he had built through years of culinary dedication. His case became a minor cause célèbre highlighting the power imbalance between independent entrepreneurs and corporate legal departments, illustrating how trademark law could be weaponised against small businesses even where no genuine market confusion existed.
