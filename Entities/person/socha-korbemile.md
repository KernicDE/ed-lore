Socha Korbemile began her documented involvement with the Canonn Interstellar Research Group as a member of one of the galaxy's most prominent independent scientific communities, participating in investigations that pushed the boundaries of human knowledge about alien phenomena and anomalous stellar objects. Her initial contributions appeared consistent with the organisation's ethos of collaborative discovery and open information sharing. However, subsequent investigations revealed a more complex relationship between Korbemile and external interests seeking to exploit Canonn's research for purposes that diverged from the group's scientific mission.

By early 3302, evidence emerged identifying Korbemile as an internal leak who had provided sensitive information to Varati-based groups connected to Dr Huros, a militant advocate for alien life forms. Her activities culminated in the sabotage of another pilot's vessel, an act that betrayed both professional trust and the safety norms governing independent scientific expeditions. Korbemile's case became a cautionary tale within research communities about the vulnerability of open organisations to infiltration by actors with agendas that conflicted with institutional values.
