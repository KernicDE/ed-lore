# Barnacle Meta-Alloy Crisis

## Summary
In January 3302, Commander Octo discovered alien "barnacle" structures on Merope 5c in the Pleiades Nebula. These structures produced meta-alloys—a material unknown to human science and the only substance capable of repairing Thargoid-corroded starport systems. The discovery triggered a superpower race for control of barnacle sites, with the Federation deploying battle cruisers to Merope and the Empire launching exploration campaigns to find additional sources. The barnacles became both a scientific marvel and a strategic flashpoint.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Discovery (3302-01-15)
Dr Arcanonn of the Canonn Interstellar Research Group confirmed the discovery of barnacles on at least two planets in the Pleiades Nebula. The structures produced meta-alloys, emitted sounds similar to Unknown Artefacts, and bore mysterious markings. Ships near barnacles experienced malfunctions, and crashed vessels were found near many sites. The Canonn launched a systematic study while superpowers quietly assessed the strategic implications.

### Phase 2: Superpower Race (3302-07-14)
The Federation stationed battle cruisers at barnacle sites in Merope, treating the region as occupied territory. The Empire responded by launching a campaign to find additional barnacles, asking pilots to deliver exploration data to Bauschinger City. The official reason was scientific, but the timing made the military motive obvious. Meta-alloys became enormously valuable on commodity markets as starports began experiencing Thargoid corrosion. Control of barnacle sites meant control of the only known repair material.

### Phase 3: Thargoid Reassertion (3305-07-18)
Thargoid forces returned to the Pleiades in large numbers, directly assaulting human installations. Professor Palin's research centre in Maia came under attack, forcing evacuation of his megaship Carson's Spring. The Thargoids were clearly asserting ownership of the region where their barnacles grew. Human claims of sovereignty over Pleiades barnacle sites became untenable as Thargoid vessels patrolled the nebula in force.

## Modern Relevance
The barnacle crisis established the Pleiades as the primary theatre of human-Thargoid conflict. Meta-alloys remain strategically vital, with supply determining which starports can be repaired after Thargoid attack. The superpower competition for barnacle access also demonstrated how alien resources drive human conflict—a pattern repeated with Guardian technology and later with Thargoid Titan research. Whether barnacles are Thargoid farms, mining installations, or something else entirely remains one of the unanswered questions of xeno-biology.
