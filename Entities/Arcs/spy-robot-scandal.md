# Spy Robot Scandal

## Summary
The spy robot scandal exposed one of the most brazen corporate surveillance operations in recent Federal history. Mars Tribune owner Kingsley Cordova blackmailed an Achilles Corporation robotics engineer into installing surveillance programs on PA912 household robots belonging to well-known figures in the Sol system. The accumulated data was relayed to the Cordova Group and published in the Mars Tribune, violating the privacy of some of the Federation's most prominent citizens.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Whistleblower (3304-11-15)
The Federal Times published a bombshell report from an anonymous Achilles Corporation engineer who claimed she was blackmailed by Kingsley Cordova into installing surveillance programs on PA912 robots. The robots, common household assistants in the Sol system, had been compromised to collect private data from their owners—politicians, celebrities, and business leaders. Chief Editor Lena Ravenhill's reporting revealed the direct connection between the Cordova Group and the spy robots, confirming large-scale corporate blackmail and surveillance.

### Phase 2: The Kill Switch (3304-11-22)
Achilles Corporation responded by releasing a coreware update containing a "kill switch" that remotely deleted the spy program from all affected PA912 robots. The company assured customers that the risk of private information misuse had been eliminated. The Mars Tribune, its reputation destroyed by the scandal, closed its doors. Kingsley Cordova faced legal consequences, though the full extent of the data breach and its political implications remained partially classified.

## Modern Relevance
The spy robot scandal remains a cautionary tale about corporate surveillance in an age of ubiquitous robotics. It exposed vulnerabilities in consumer technology that could be exploited by unscrupulous actors, and highlighted the tension between media freedom and privacy rights. Achilles Corporation's remote kill switch solution, while effective, also raised questions about how much control manufacturers maintain over devices after purchase.
