# Nova Imperium Crisis

## Summary
The Nova Imperium was a radical isolationist faction within the Empire that rejected Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval's policy of limited cooperation with the Federation and Alliance. Led by the charismatic Imperator Kaeso Mordanticus and championing Hadrian Duval—a distant relative of the Emperor—as the rightful heir, the movement grew rapidly among disaffected Imperial citizens. What began as political dissent escalated into armed insurrection, culminating in the Battle of Paresa and one of the most violent purges in Imperial history.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Manifesto (3304-12-18)
Imperator Kaeso Mordanticus published the Nova Imperium manifesto, calling for complete Imperial isolation from other superpowers and the installation of Hadrian Duval as Emperor. The movement attracted veterans, conservative senators, and citizens alienated by Arissa's pragmatic foreign policy. Mordanticus framed cooperation with the Federation as contamination of Imperial purity, drawing on deep-seated traditions of Imperial exceptionalism.

### Phase 2: The Battle of Paresa (3305-01-17)
Nova Imperium forces clashed with Imperial Navy units led by Yupini Limited in the Paresa system. Senator Denton Patreus directed the Imperial response, warning that the Empire would not tolerate insurrection. After days of fighting, Nova Imperium was decisively defeated. Mordanticus was captured alive, a decision that would prove fatal for him and many others.

### Phase 3: Blood on the Senate Floor (3305-01-25)
Mordanticus was brought before the Imperial Senate in shackles, still defiant. Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval entered attended by Senator Patreus, who approached Mordanticus and executed him with his personal sidearm. The Imperial Guard then opened fire, gunning down senators Pal Vespasian and Eloise Winterstone along with others who had supported the isolationist cause. Arissa stood over the body and declared: "There is no Nova Imperium. There is only the Empire." Mass arrests and further executions followed across Capitol.

## Modern Relevance
The Nova Imperium crisis demonstrated the brutal efficiency with which the Empire suppresses internal dissent. Patreus's personal execution of Mordanticus and the subsequent Senate massacre sent an unmistakable message: challenges to Imperial unity are met with lethal force. Hadrian Duval's survival—despite his association with the movement—preserved a potential rival to Arissa's line, one that would resurface in later political calculations. The crisis also exposed the depth of isolationist sentiment within the Empire, a current that continues to influence Imperial foreign policy.
