# Imperial Succession Crisis

## Summary
The brewing political crisis surrounding Emperor Hengist Duval's failing health. With the Emperor unable to rule and no clear succession plan, Imperial power brokers manoeuvre for position while Chancellor Blaine struggles to maintain order.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Patreus Challenges Blaine (3301-03-12)
Senator Patreus publicly demands access to the Emperor's medical records, arguing that the Senate has a right to know whether Hengist will recover. His challenge is both a genuine concern for Imperial stability and a calculated move to undermine Blaine's authority.

### Phase 2: Blaine Holds the Line (3301-03-14)
Chancellor Blaine refuses the request, citing national security and the Emperor's privacy. He insists succession will be addressed "when the situation calls for it" — transparent stalling that satisfies no one. The Senate's paralysis becomes increasingly dangerous as the Emperor's condition worsens.

## Modern Relevance
The succession crisis is the ticking time bomb at the heart of Imperial politics in 3301. Blaine's weakness, Patreus's ambition, and the absence of a clear heir create a volatile situation that will explode when the Emperor eventually dies. The crisis tests the resilience of Imperial institutions and reveals how dependent the Empire is on a single monarchical figure. Arissa Lavigny-Duval's eventual ascension to the throne will resolve this immediate crisis, but the underlying fragility of Imperial succession remains unresolved for years.
