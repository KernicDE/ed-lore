# Cayutorme Conflict

## Summary
The Cayutorme Syndicate was a mercenary fleet operating as a criminal organisation in the Cayutorme system. Led by Kyle Kurowski, the syndicate maintained a formidable armada of independent pilots who rejected Imperial authority. In November 3301, the Empire issued an ultimatum: disband or face military action. When the deadline passed, Imperial naval vessels entered the system, triggering open combat between the Imperial Navy and the syndicate's mercenary forces.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Ultimatum (3301-11-04)
The Empire demanded the Cayutorme Syndicate submit to inspection and disbandment. The syndicate refused. Kyle Kurowski, speaking from an undisclosed location, rejected Imperial authority and appealed to independent pilots to support the syndicate's cause. Admiral Lucius Lavigny—the Emperor's second cousin—opened the Imperial war chest to mercenaries willing to support the Imperial cause, framing the conflict as law enforcement against criminal elements.

### Phase 2: Open Conflict (3301-11-05)
Imperial naval vessels entered the Cayutorme system and engaged the syndicate fleet. Kurowski denied reports that independent pilots were fighting for both sides, insisting that those who supported the syndicate did so out of conviction rather than profit. The Imperial war chest funded mercenaries who reinforced naval units. The fighting was intense, with both sides suffering casualties. Admiral Lavigny urged commanders to support the Imperial cause, warning that if the syndicate was permitted to continue, more systems would suffer.

### Phase 3: Order Restored (3301-11-11)
Imperial forces secured the Cayutorme system and declared order restored. The syndicate's fleet was dispersed or destroyed. The Empire installed compliant local administration and established a permanent naval presence to prevent resurgence. Kyle Kurowski's fate remained unclear—some reports claimed he was killed in the fighting, while others suggested he escaped to continue resistance elsewhere. The conflict demonstrated the Empire's willingness to project military force against organised criminal elements operating beyond its formal borders.

## Modern Relevance
The Cayutorme Conflict established the Imperial template for handling organised criminal fleets: issue ultimatum, deploy overwhelming force, and install permanent security presence. This approach would be repeated in later conflicts against pirate fleets and criminal syndicates. The conflict also highlighted the ambiguous status of "independent pilots"—were they mercenaries, criminals, or legitimate political actors? The Empire's answer was clear: those who rejected Imperial authority were enemies to be destroyed.
