# Antares Mystery

## Summary
The Highliner Antares was to be the first of a new class of fast, luxurious passenger liners—making trips in hours rather than weeks. During its maiden voyage in the Sirius system in 3251, the ship vanished with many celebrity guests aboard. The official report cited a component failure causing fire and explosion during jump initiation, but no confirmed wreckage was ever found. The mystery spawned decades of conspiracy theories, multiple claims of discovery, and ultimately shaped hyperdrive safety regulations across human space.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Incident (3251-01-01)
The Antares departed on its maiden voyage from Sirius, carrying celebrity passengers and representing a leap forward in passenger transport technology. It was the first ship to use a production version of a new hyperdrive type—a predecessor to modern fast drives. During jump initiation, something went catastrophically wrong. The ship vanished from sensors. Federal Times reporting from the era described the departure with haunting prescience: "We can be sure that the great Highliner Antares is now in their company"—referring to the "graveyard of lost ships."

### Phase 2: Investigations (3301-05-27)
Historian Sima Kalhana revisited the incident in a GalNet series, noting that until Starship One's disappearance, the Antares was considered the most infamous spacegoing accident in history. The official investigation concluded that a single component failure during jump initiation caused a small fire that triggered a massive explosion and consequent misjump. However, the lack of definitive cause combined with no confirmed wreckage fueled conspiracy theories. The incident was later linked to the death of a Vice President, suggesting possible sabotage or cover-up.

### Phase 3: Wreckage Claims (3302-01-13)
Sirius Corporation announced confirmation of Antares wreckage discovery, raising hopes that the mystery would finally be solved. However, subsequent analysis cast doubt on whether the recovered debris actually belonged to the lost liner. Multiple claims of discovery had been made over the decades, none definitively proven. Sirius Corporation's involvement was particularly fraught given that the Antares was their flagship project, and any admission of design flaw would have damaged their reputation and triggered massive liability claims.

## Modern Relevance
The Antares incident shaped hyperdrive safety standards for generations. The final safety report found insufficient precautions in internal drive systems, leading to enhanced safety features that measurably reduced ship disappearances in subsequent decades. Some analysts argue that the deaths aboard the Antares effectively saved more lives than they cost by forcing safety improvements. The unresolved nature of the disappearance—no confirmed wreckage, questionable official explanations—also established a template for spacegoing mysteries that would be repeated with Starship One.
