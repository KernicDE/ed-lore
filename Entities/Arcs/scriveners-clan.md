# Scriveners Clan

## Summary
The Scriveners Clan is one of several nomadic dredger tribes that roam the galaxy in converted bulk cruisers, salvaging wrecks and accumulating knowledge in their "Knowledge Core." In 3307, a Scriveners dredger discovered the Hesperus—a 200-year-old "ghost ship" once owned by Azimuth Biochemicals—in the Perseus Dark Region. The clan's salvage operation stripped the megaship's technology and data before they departed abruptly, leaving behind a mostly intact hull that became a focal point for scientific and corporate claims.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Discovery (3307-04-01)
The Scriveners Clan's dredger located the Hesperus in the Perseus Dark Region KC-V c2-2 system. The megaship, launched in 3113 by Azimuth Biochemicals, had been overtaken by saboteurs from rival Pharmasapien during a secret research mission. One of its secondary vessels, the Anaconda Proteus, had escaped to a Guardian-Thargoid battlefield where its crew conducted research before dying. The Hesperus itself drifted for two centuries until the Scriveners found it.

### Phase 2: Salvage Operation (3307-07-09)
The Scriveners began systematically stripping the Hesperus of materials and data. Their dredger—a massive bulk cruiser adapted for salvage—consumed the megaship's innards while the clan extracted whatever information survived in its systems. Thousands of curious travellers visited the site, drawn by the mystery of the ghost ship and its connection to Azimuth Biochemicals. Erik Gunnarson of the Wallglass Investigations Agency speculated that the Scriveners were particularly interested in any data related to the Guardian-Thargoid war.

### Phase 3: Resistance and Departure (3307-10-22)
The Scriveners abandoned the salvage operation prematurely, leaving behind a mostly intact hull. Gunnarson theorised they departed either because the attention from thousands of visitors exceeded what the secretive tribe could tolerate, or because they detected richer pickings elsewhere—either fresh resources or new information for their Knowledge Core. Orion University later attempted to claim the site for research purposes, but the Scriveners' departure left questions about what they had extracted and where their dredger traveled next.

## Modern Relevance
The Scriveners Clan represents a largely unexplored frontier culture—nomadic societies living in space for generations, with their own customs, technologies, and priorities. Their salvage of the Hesperus demonstrated how these tribes can inadvertently shape historical understanding by recovering and hoarding data from lost vessels. The appearance of other dredger clans, such as the Blue Viper Club at Kumo City, suggests these nomadic societies may play increasingly important roles in frontier politics and archaeology.
