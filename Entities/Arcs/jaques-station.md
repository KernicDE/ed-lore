# Jaques Station

## Summary
Jaques Station, a mobile Orbis starport operated by the eccentric cyborg bartender Jaques, represented one of the most ambitious engineering projects in human history. In 3302, the station attempted a long-range jump toward Beagle Point but suffered a catastrophic misjump, stranding it 22,000 light-years from Sol in an uncharted region later named Colonia. The subsequent rescue and colonisation effort transformed a disaster into one of the galaxy's most remarkable frontier settlements.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Disappearance (3302-05-05)
Jaques announced his intention to jump the station to Beagle Point, the farthest reachable system from Sol. During the attempt, the station's drives malfunctioned and it vanished from sensors. For weeks, the galactic community assumed the worst—that the station and its inhabitants were lost forever.

### Phase 2: Rediscovery (3302-06-28)
Independent explorers located Jaques Station in what would become known as the Colonia system, deep in the galactic coreward regions. The station had survived but was damaged, and Jaques requested emergency supplies. The discovery triggered a massive community response, with the Fuel Rats and other organisations coordinating rescue efforts. This event marked the beginning of permanent human settlement in the region.

### Phase 3: Colonia Bridge (3307-11-04)
Brewer Corporation launched the Colonia Bridge project, constructing a chain of megaships and starports to create a stable travel route between the core systems and Colonia. The initiative dramatically reduced travel time and cemented Colonia's status as a viable deep-space civilization, complete its own economy, politics, and culture.

## Modern Relevance
Jaques Station stands as proof that human settlement can thrive even in the most remote regions of the galaxy. The Colonia region, born from a navigational accident, now hosts thousands of pilots and multiple factions. The Colonia Bridge remains a vital artery for trade and exploration, and the station itself continues to serve as the region's cultural and economic hub.
