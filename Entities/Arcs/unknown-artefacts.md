# Unknown Artefacts

## Summary
The Unknown Artefacts—later identified as Thargoid probes—were among the first concrete evidence of alien intelligence encountered by modern humanity. Recovered from Federal Navy convoys and studied intensively by Dr Arcanonn's Canonn research team, these mysterious objects emitted distorted signals, caused equipment malfunctions, and ultimately revealed themselves as scouting devices for the Thargoid civilization.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Discovery (3301-05-04)
Unknown Artefacts first appeared in Federal Navy convoys, their origin and purpose completely mysterious. Independent pilots began collecting them, and the Canonn Interstellar Research Group launched a systematic study. The objects appeared to be biological-mechanical hybrids, unlike anything in human technology.

### Phase 2: The Morse Code Breakthrough (3301-07-10)
Dr Arcanonn's team discovered that the artefacts emitted a distorted but audible signal matching Morse code—the ancient Earth communication system. Even more remarkably, the signal broadcast the identity of the nearest celestial body when released into free space. This suggested either human involvement in their creation or advanced alien knowledge of human technology. Dr Arcanonn publicly called on the Federal Government to release any classified information about the artefacts.

### Phase 3: Corrosive Effects (3301-12-21)
As more artefacts were brought into populated systems, a disturbing pattern emerged: the devices caused progressive equipment malfunctions and corrosion in starport systems. Stations near artefact concentrations began experiencing technical failures. The market value of Unknown Artefacts spiked dramatically as someone—unknown—began paying heavily for them. These effects were later understood as the artefacts' function as Thargoid scouting probes, mapping human infrastructure and reporting back.

## Modern Relevance
The Unknown Artefact saga was humanity's first direct encounter with active alien technology and served as a warning of the Thargoid presence that would soon escalate into open conflict. The Canonn research group's open-science approach to studying the artefacts established a model for how independent researchers could contribute to xeno-understanding, bypassing government secrecy.
