# Halsey Presidency

## Summary
Jasmina Halsey's presidency was marked by idealistic governance and ambitious frontier policies, but it ended abruptly when Starship One—her presidential vessel—disappeared during a tour of Federal systems in May 3301. Officially ruled a mechanical failure, the incident spawned conspiracy theories, political infighting, and eventually the rise of Zachary Hudson to the presidency. Halsey's unexpected survival and return years later transformed her from a tragic figure into an advocate for inter-superpower unity.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Presidency (3301-03-16)
Jasmina Halsey assumed the Federal presidency with an agenda focused on social welfare and frontier development. Her administration pursued policies that drew both praise and criticism, with Shadow President Felicia Winters supporting her initiatives while Zachary Hudson emerged as a vocal opponent. Halsey announced an ambitious tour of frontier systems to demonstrate Federal commitment to outer colonies.

### Phase 2: The Disappearance (3301-05-26)
Starship One vanished during the frontier tour. The official investigation concluded mechanical failure, but conflicting accounts emerged immediately. Felicia Winters insisted on mechanical failure from the beginning, while Zachary Hudson publicly claimed sabotage—until he became President himself, at which point his position shifted. Groups like the Knights of the Black suspected an alien threat or Federal cover-up. The disappearance triggered a political crisis that ultimately propelled Hudson into the presidency.

### Phase 3: The Return (3307-01-20)
In a shocking development, Jasmina Halsey reappeared years later, having survived the Starship One disaster. She provided testimony about the event and suggested motivations behind the assassination attempt. Rather than returning to partisan politics, Halsey became a Federal Ambassador, using her experience to advocate for inter-superpower cooperation. Her transformation from pragmatic politician to diplomatic bridge-builder was one of the more remarkable political rehabilitations in Federal history.

## Modern Relevance
The Halsey presidency and its dramatic conclusion exposed deep fractures in Federal political culture. The conflicting explanations for Starship One's loss—mechanical failure versus sabotage versus alien attack—reflected broader tensions between transparency and security. Halsey's eventual return as an ambassador rather than a partisan fighter offered a model for how political figures could transcend superpower divisions, a particularly relevant example during the era of the Galactic Summit.
