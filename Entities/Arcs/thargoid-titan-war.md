# Thargoid Titan War

## Summary
The Thargoid Titan War represents the most severe phase of human-Thargoid conflict. Massive Thargoid motherships—called Titans—invaded human space, deploying Maelstroms that disrupted navigation and communications. The Thargoids abducted thousands of humans, storing them in bio-storage capsules aboard the Titans. Human forces, led by Aegis and supported by independent pilots, developed increasingly sophisticated countermeasures including pulse wave xeno scanners and sub-surface extraction missiles. The war has no clear end in sight, with both sides suffering heavy losses and the Thargoids demonstrating capabilities that continue to exceed human understanding.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Invasion (3309-05-01)
Thargoid Titans—massive motherships of unprecedented scale—entered human space in force. They deployed Maelstroms, zone-wide disruptions that made navigation hazardous and communication unreliable. Starports came under direct assault. Aegis and Azimuth Biotech competed for Titan samples, each seeking to develop countermeasures first. The scale of the invasion exceeded anything in previous conflicts, suggesting the Thargoids had escalated from probing attacks to total war.

### Phase 2: Captives (3309-10-10)
Rescue teams made a horrifying discovery: the Thargoids were abducting humans and storing them in bio-storage capsules aboard the Titans. Using upgraded pulse wave xeno scanners and sub-surface extraction missiles, pilots began rescuing captives. Dr Himari Grey observed the extraction procedures: "They were suspended within a chemically complex gel, not unlike amniotic fluid, with tubes supplying breathable air and basic nutrients." Recovered individuals remembered little, though some described an "overwhelming urge to escape their ship" before blacking out. The capsules were designed to sustain oxygen-breathing life indefinitely, raising chilling questions about Thargoid intentions.

### Phase 3: Countermeasures (3309-12-01)
Human forces surrounded the Titan Taranis and developed increasingly effective anti-xeno tactics. Aegis received materials for xeno research while Azimuth refocused on Titan analysis. The Kumo Crew organized anti-xeno militias in the California Nebula, though their attempts to exploit the crisis for territorial gain were repelled. Concerns were raised about Titan returnees—those rescued from captivity—and whether they could be fully cleared of Thargoid influence. The war settled into a grinding attrition, with neither side able to achieve decisive victory.

## Modern Relevance
The Titan War has fundamentally changed how humanity wages war. The abduction of human captives—kept alive in alien stasis—introduced a hostage dimension previously absent from the conflict. The development of extraction technologies demonstrated human ingenuity under pressure, but also revealed how dependent defense has become on independent pilots rather than formal military forces. Whether the Thargoids intend to use captives for research, hybridisation, or something else entirely remains unknown, making the psychological cost of the war as significant as its military toll.
