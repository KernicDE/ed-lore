# Cerberus Plague

## Summary
The Cerberus Plague was a deadly infectious disease that emerged at Newton Dock in the BD-02 4304 system in July 3301. Named for the unusual three-pronged structure of the phage-like organism, the plague spread rapidly through populated systems and threatened to become a galaxy-wide pandemic. The outbreak was ultimately contained when medical personnel discovered that Ceremonial Heike Tea—offered originally just to lift patients' spirits—contained ingredients that could cure the pathogen.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Outbreak (3301-07-14)
The plague first appeared at Newton Dock in BD-02 4304, where Chief Medical Officer Carl Simmons coordinated containment efforts. Local authorities and independent pilots worked to slow the outbreak, but isolated incidents were soon confirmed in Dajoar, Eleu, and 20 Ophiuchi—believed to be travellers who had left BD-02 4304 before restrictions were imposed. Simmons called on independent pilots to help prevent full outbreaks in these new locations.

### Phase 2: Spread (3301-07-20)
Three separate containment operations were initiated as the disease threatened to escape control. The exact transmission vector remained unknown, and medical teams took nothing for granted. The situation grew increasingly desperate as established treatment methods proved ineffective against the novel pathogen.

### Phase 3: The Cure (3301-07-29)
At Hart Station in the Bast system, a breakthrough occurred by accident. Patients offered Ceremonial Heike Tea showed unexpected improvement. When other patients experienced similar recoveries, medical teams ran tests and sent samples to Carl Simmons at Newton Dock. He confirmed that the tea contained ingredients capable of curing those afflicted with the pathogen. The challenge then became gathering sufficient quantities, as the tea was rare and only found in Heike. Mass production and distribution across affected systems ultimately halted the outbreak.

## Modern Relevance
The Cerberus Plague demonstrated both the vulnerability of densely populated systems to infectious disease and the power of coordinated community response. The role of independent pilots in delivering medicines and the accidental discovery of a traditional beverage as a cure highlighted how unconventional solutions can emerge during crises. The plague also exposed gaps in inter-system quarantine protocols that were later strengthened.
