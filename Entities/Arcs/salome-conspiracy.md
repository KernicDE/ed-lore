# Salomé Conspiracy

## Summary
The Salomé Conspiracy was one of the most enigmatic political mysteries of the early 3300s. Senator Kahina Tijani Loren of the Prism system disappeared in 3301 during her controversial Federation tour. When she resurfaced, it was not as an Imperial politician but as Commander Salomé—a revolutionary figure linked to multiple underground organisations including the Children of Raxxla, the Dark Wheel, Emperor's Dawn, and the Phoenix Group. Her campaign of destabilisation culminated in an assassination attempt against Admiral Denton Patreus, her arrest, trial, and eventual conviction.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Disappearance (3301-05-06)
Senator Loren vanished in the Cemiess system following her return from Federation space. The Children of Raxxla attempted to destabilise the system's economy to force authorities to reveal her whereabouts. For months, her fate remained unknown. Imperial media speculated about everything from kidnapping to voluntary defection. The truth—that she had reinvented herself as a revolutionary operative—would only emerge much later.

### Phase 2: The Salomé Identity (3302-09-19)
Imperial authorities revealed that Lady Kahina had been operating under the alias Commander Salomé, linked to revolutionary organisations including Emperor's Dawn, the Children of Raxxla, the Phoenix Group, and the Dark Wheel. She had been implicated in the attempted assassination of Admiral Denton Patreus. Princess Aisling Duval condemned her, saying her "true colours have been revealed." The Imperial Citizen newspaper denounced her as "dangerously unstable and an irresponsible warmonger." Loren was moved to Achenar to await trial.

### Phase 3: Arrest and Trial (3302-10-14)
Kahina Loren was stripped of her senatorial rank and tried for her crimes. She was found guilty and imprisoned. Before her arrest, however, she had indicated that three specific sectors—the Formidine Rift, the Conflux, and Hawkin's Gap—were significant. The Children of Raxxla later confirmed that exploration data supported the existence of a covert mission called the "Dynasty Project" in these regions during the 3270s. Some believed this was why she was killed—or why authorities wanted her silenced.

## Modern Relevance
The Salomé Conspiracy blurs the line between political dissident and terrorist, raising questions about how revolutionary movements are categorised by those in power. Loren's claims about the Dynasty Project were partially validated by later discoveries, suggesting she possessed genuine knowledge that threatened powerful interests. Her connections to multiple secret societies also hinted at a network of resistance operating beneath the surface of galactic politics—a network that may have survived her imprisonment and continues to influence events.
