# Galactic Summit

## Summary
The Galactic Summit represented the most ambitious diplomatic initiative in recent history—an attempt to bring Alliance, Empire, and Federation leaders together for face-to-face negotiations on the Thargoid threat, terrorism, and inter-superpower cooperation. Proposed by Utopia and ultimately hosted by Sirius Corporation in the Sirius system, the summit drew unprecedented attendance including Chancellor Anders Blaine, Prime Minister Edmund Mahon, President Zachary Hudson, Shadow President Felicia Winters, and Princess Aisling Duval. After weeks of careful negotiation, the conference ended in catastrophe when the Neo-Marlinist Liberation Army launched coordinated terrorist attacks across nine starports, an atrocity they called the "Nine Martyrs."

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Proposal (3307-01-01)
Utopia invited all three superpowers to a Galactic Summit, proposing a neutral forum for addressing shared threats. Sirius Corporation and Utopia competed to host the event, with Sirius ultimately securing the bid. The very idea of superpower leaders meeting face-to-face was historic—no such conference had ever occurred. Preparations involved complex security arrangements, rare goods procurement for diplomatic functions, and the issuance of temporary permits for the Sirius system.

### Phase 2: Historic Meetings (3307-02-26)
Leaders convened at Patterson Enterprise in the Sirius system. Chancellor Blaine and Prime Minister Mahon delivered optimistic opening statements, while President Hudson was characteristically curt, reminding delegates that billions of lives depended on their decisions. Behind the scenes, ambassadorial staff hammered out agendas for three weeks of negotiations. Notable absences included Federal Ambassador Jordan Rochester, whose brother's criminal charges created diplomatic awkwardness with his ex-fiancée Princess Aisling Duval. Archon Delaine of the Kumo Crew gatecrashed proceedings, adding criminal element chaos to an already tense environment.

### Phase 3: Nine Martyrs (3307-03-11)
The NMLA struck simultaneously across nine systems including the superpower capitals of Achenar, Alioth, and Sol. Thousands died in the bombings. The terrorist known as Theta Seven broadcast a manifesto claiming the attacks as retaliation for Imperial persecution of Marlinists. Sirius Corporation cancelled the summit immediately, fearing Patterson Enterprise would be the next target. The Marlinist Parliament disavowed the extremists, but speculation that NMLA activists had hidden among Marlinist refugees fleeing to Alliance and Federation space poisoned inter-superpower trust.

## Modern Relevance
The Galactic Summit's failure demonstrated how effectively terrorism could derail even the most promising diplomatic initiatives. The Nine Martyrs attack reshaped security policy across all three superpowers, leading to stricter refugee screening and expanded surveillance powers. The collapse of the summit also meant the Sirius Treaty—intended to formalise anti-Thargoid cooperation—was never completed, leaving superpowers to respond to the alien threat without coordinated strategy. The tragedy underscored the vulnerability of concentrated diplomatic gatherings to asymmetric attack.
