A new and highly organised pirate threat has emerged in the core systems under the command of a rogue pirate admiral known only as "Lady Zi." Unlike the opportunistic piracy typically seen in the Bubble, Lady Zi's operations display military-level coordination and technical sophistication.

The first confirmed attacks occurred on 3312-04-23, when coordinated raids hit military outposts across multiple core systems. Investigators noted that the raids followed patterns usually reserved for military reconnaissance — probing response times, defensive weak points, and coordination speeds between system security and independent pilots. Digital malware displaying a distinctive twin-axe motif disrupted security networks during the raids, now believed to be Lady Zi's calling card.

The situation escalated dramatically on 3312-05-11 with a full-scale assault on starport facilities across the Lhou Mans system. A coalition of pirate ships hit multiple ports simultaneously, triggering mass civilian evacuations. Evidence gathered by Vox Galactica crime correspondent Mira Bukowski linked the twin-axe motif to Lady Zi through underworld sources, revealing a single criminal syndicate directing a unified coalition fleet.

By 3312-05-14, security forces and independent combat pilots had successfully defended rescue efforts and driven out the remaining criminals. The evacuation was deemed a success, with civilians moved to safer locations. However, analysts now believe the Lhou Mans attack was merely a "test run" — a probing exercise designed to test system defences in preparation for larger, bolder coordinated operations.

The crisis also provided an unexpected platform for Zorgon Peterson's new Lynx Highliner and Mk II cabin modules, which reportedly outperformed expectations under extreme evacuation conditions. Lhou Mans authorities are now calling for pilots to aid repair efforts at Ryman Enterprise.
