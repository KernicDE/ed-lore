# Patreus Campaigns

## Summary
Senator Denton Patreus's early political and military initiatives in 3301, combining economic expansion through debt-slavery with hawkish security rhetoric. These campaigns establish Patreus as a major power player and preview his broader ambitions for Imperial expansion.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Durius Assimilation (3301-02-16)
Patreus, in partnership with Senator Torval, facilitates the transfer of over 12,000 Imperial Slaves from Durius as part of a debt relief programme. The citizens "voluntarily" enter slavery to repay system debts.

### Phase 2: Security Rhetoric (3301-02-17)
Patreus delivers a powerful speech on Eotienses, framing Imperial expansion as a moral duty. He references the Emperor's failing health and targets Falisci as a threat to Imperial stability.

## Modern Relevance
Patreus's methods — debt-slavery, strategic alliances with fellow senators, and moralistic rhetoric about security — become the foundation of his power base. His early targeting of Falisci foreshadows future Imperial military campaigns. The Patreus-Torval alliance proves enduring and shapes Imperial economic policy for years.
