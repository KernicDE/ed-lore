# Distant Worlds 3

## Summary
Distant Worlds 3 continued the tradition of massive community exploration expeditions that began with earlier ventures in 3305. Organised by Deep Space Surveys with support from the Distant Worlds working group, this expedition focused on constructing new deep-space infrastructure in seldom-charted regions far beyond established routes. Building on the success of Explorers' Anchorage—constructed during Distant Worlds II—the initiative invited independent pilots to deliver construction materials in exchange for rewards including pre-engineered ship modules.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Preparation (3312-01-08)
The Distant Worlds working group issued a call for mined and manufactured materials to support construction of a new deep-space facility. Hermine Delarue, Executive Operative for Deep Space Surveys, cited the organisation's experience supporting Distant Worlds II and the construction of Explorers' Anchorage as precedent. Quartermaster Timotheus Haddard prioritised bromellite, power generators, and thermal cooling units. Contributors were offered a class 5 pre-engineered cargo rack as incentive. The expedition was scheduled to depart on January 18th.

### Phase 2: Fleet Departure (3312-01-18)
Hundreds of independent pilots departed from staging areas, heading toward the galactic northeast and beyond. The fleet composition ranged from nimble exploration vessels to heavy-lift transports carrying construction materials. The journey required navigating through the Empyrean Straits and other challenging regions where few pilots had traveled. Communication with the core systems became increasingly delayed as the fleet pushed beyond relay network range.

### Phase 3: Deep Space Survey (3312-03-19)
The expedition reached Beagle Point—the farthest reachable system from Sol—and began systematic survey operations. From there, the fleet departed to continue deep-space surveys in uncharted regions, cataloguing stellar phenomena and planetary bodies. The construction materials delivered during preparation were used to establish the primary framework for a new frontier outpost, intended to support future exploration far beyond current boundaries.

## Modern Relevance
Distant Worlds 3 established a precedent for community-funded deep-space infrastructure that will likely be replicated by future expeditions. The crowdfunding model—pilots delivering materials in exchange for unique rewards—demonstrated how frontier construction could bypass traditional corporate or superpower funding. The resulting outposts become permanent additions to the human presence in remote space, creating stepping stones for ever-more-distant exploration.
