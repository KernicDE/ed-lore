## Description

The Salvation-Azimuth arc is one of the darkest chapters in the Second Thargoid War, centred on Dr. Caleb Wycherley — a scientist who survived for over two centuries through progenitor cell treatments and operated under the aliases "Salvation" and "The Witch." As vice-president of research at Azimuth Biochemicals during the First Thargoid War, Wycherley had assisted INRA in developing the mycoid bioweapon. In 3307, he resurfaced with a new agenda: to supplant Aegis and destroy the Thargoids using Guardian-derived technology, regardless of the human cost.

## Narrative

Wycherley first made contact with independent pilots in April 3307 through the ghost ship Adamastor in the Chukchan system. Using the corporate front Taurus Mining Ventures, he directed pilots to trace the Adamastor's encrypted signal to its sister ship, the Hesperus, and eventually to the Proteus — an Azimuth expedition vessel found at an ancient Guardian-Thargoid battlefield. The technology recovered from the Proteus became the foundation for Salvation's anti-xeno superweapon: a Guardian-derived electromagnetic pulse device capable of disabling Thargoid vessels across an entire system.

Salvation's methods were ruthlessly effective and ethically abhorrent. In September 3307, he covertly seeded the inhabited Cornsar system with Guardian technology fragments to lure Thargoids into an invasion, then deployed his prototype superweapon to destroy them — using a civilian population as bait. The weapon worked, and the superpowers began redirecting resources from Aegis to Salvation's operation. When Admiral Aden Tanner attempted to investigate Salvation's activities by commandeering the Aegis megaship Musashi and raiding Hind Mine, he was defeated by pilots supporting Salvation and subsequently court-martialed.

The full extent of Salvation's crimes emerged gradually. In June 3307, the Aegis megaship Alexandria was sabotaged and its cargo of Guardian artefacts stolen by mercenaries working for "The Witch" — later confirmed to be Wycherley himself. In August 3307, abandoned Project Seraph facilities were discovered in HIP 22460 and HIP 26176, revealing that Azimuth had conducted horrific human experimentation in attempts to install human pilots into captured Thargoid scouts. In May 3308, Professor Alba Tesreau uncovered logs at INRA base Iraxon Lane proving that Salvation was Dr. Caleb Wycherley and that he had participated in Project Mycoid.

The arc culminated at HIP 22460 in August 3308. Salvation activated the Proteus Wave — a massive Guardian-derived superweapon intended to clear the system of Thargoids. The weapon initially appeared successful, but triggered a catastrophic Thargoid counter-attack of unprecedented scale. The Thargoids deployed Titan-class vessels and a counter-wave that destroyed the Bright Sentinel megaship, killing Wycherley and thousands of personnel. The battle marked a turning point in the war, escalating the conflict to a level of destruction unseen since the First Thargoid War.

Salvation's legacy is deeply contested. His supporters credit him with developing effective anti-xeno weapons and killing millions of Thargoids. His detractors — including former Aegis leadership and the families of his victims — condemn him as a mass murderer who manipulated the superpowers, sacrificed civilian populations, and ultimately provoked a Thargoid retaliation that devastated human defences. The collapse of Aegis, the radicalisation of Thargoid tactics, and the deployment of Titan-class vessels can all be traced, directly or indirectly, to Wycherley's actions.
