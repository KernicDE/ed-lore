# The Blight

## Summary
The Blight was a fast-spreading crop disease that emerged in October 3305, causing mass spoilage on key agricultural worlds across the galaxy. Dr Genevieve Kane of the Interstellar Association for Agriculture confirmed that the same strain of advanced blight was responsible for rapid plant decay on multiple planets. The pathogen resisted all established treatments, and its origin—whether natural mutation or artificial—remained hotly debated. Through coordinated scientific effort and community support, an agronomic treatment was developed that eliminated the pathogen and prevented mass starvation.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Mystery (3305-10-14)
The Interstellar Association for Agriculture confirmed that mass crop spoilage on multiple planets was caused by a new strain of advanced blight. Its origin and vectors remained unknown, and it resisted all established treatment methods. The Vandermeer Corporation offered research assistance, bringing molecular diagnostics expertise to the effort. Journalist Adalyn Cross reported that speculation ranged from chronic mismanagement to industrial sabotage, with competing factions blaming each other in every affected system.

### Phase 2: Synthetic Source (3305-10-20)
Investigation traced the blight to a synthetic contaminant, confirming it was not a natural mutation. The revelation shifted the crisis from an agricultural disaster to a potential act of industrial or biological warfare. Superpowers collaborated on the investigation while trade initiatives and charitable imports provided temporary relief. A militant group was later identified as responsible for deploying the contaminant.

### Phase 3: The Cure (3305-10-31)
The Interstellar Association for Agriculture developed an agronomic treatment that proved effective against the blight pathogen. Distribution efforts focused on the Diso and Orerve systems, where farming communities began recovering from severe crop damage. Dr Kane confirmed that the counteragent eliminated the mutated pathogen within hours. Mass production began immediately, and food deliveries replenished lost stocks across affected worlds.

## Modern Relevance
The Blight crisis demonstrated both the vulnerability of galactic food supply chains and the resilience of coordinated scientific response. The rapid development and distribution of a cure—within weeks of the outbreak—showed what interstellar collaboration could achieve under pressure. The synthetic origin of the pathogen also raised enduring questions about bioweapon proliferation and the security of agricultural infrastructure, concerns that remain relevant as tensions between superpowers continue.
