# Banki Rebellion

## Summary
A series of Federal military interventions in frontier systems beginning with Banki and BD+03 2338, where rebel forces attempted to secede or overthrow local governance. The Federation responds with overwhelming naval force, then expands operations to pursue rebels into neighbouring systems including Nijotec and Polahukuna.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Counter-Offensive (3301-02-13)
Federal Navy mobilises around Banki and BD+03 2338. Independent commanders are recruited to support loyalist forces. A civilian evacuation warning is issued.

### Phase 2: Victory Declaration (3301-02-15)
Admiral Vincent confidently declares the rebellion will be crushed swiftly. President Halsey's administration is portrayed as distracted by the death of Vice President Smeaton.

### Phase 3: Expansion (3301-02-20)
The conflict expands beyond the original systems. Halsey orders an assault on Nijotec, targeting the Nijotec Syndicate. Vincent announces plans to pursue Polahukuna Raiders into their home system.

## Modern Relevance
The Banki Rebellion establishes the template for Federal military intervention in frontier systems: swift force, pursuit of rebels into their territories, and moral rhetoric about "liberation." This pattern repeats throughout the decade and becomes a source of both Federal pride and frontier resentment. The doctrine of "no sanctuary" for rebels contributes to later accusations of Federal imperialism.
