# Guardian-Ancient Research

## Summary
The Guardians were an advanced alien civilization that fought a devastating war with the Thargoids over a million years ago, leaving behind ruins and technology scattered across the galaxy. Beginning in 3303, independent explorers and researchers—led by figures like Ram Tah—began systematically uncovering Guardian sites, decoding their history, and recovering functional technology. The race to exploit these discoveries drew in Aegis, major corporations, and eventually triggered open corporate warfare in Guardian space.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Ruins Discovered (3303-01-01)
Independent explorers began locating Guardian ruins in remote systems, particularly in the Pleiades and surrounding regions. These sites contained obelisks, relics, and architectural remnants of a civilization that had achieved technological sophistication comparable to—or exceeding—human capabilities. Ram Tah, a leading xenoarchaeologist, published research suggesting the Guardians were not a single species but a symbiotic civilization of biological and artificial intelligences.

### Phase 2: Technology Recovery (3304-01-01)
As exploration continued, pilots discovered that Guardian materials could be used to construct advanced ship modules—hybrid technology combining human engineering with alien components. Guardian shard cannons, gauss cannons, and shield reinforcements proved particularly effective against Thargoid vessels. Aegis established research programs to weaponise these discoveries, while independent pilots traded Guardian commodities on an increasingly lucrative market.

### Phase 3: Corporate Conflict (3305-05-23)
The commercial value of Guardian commodities triggered corporate competition. Zende Partners and Segnen Exchange clashed in Synuefe EN-H d11-96, each establishing outposts and calling on independent pilots to support their competing weapons programs. Zende Partners developed an Advanced Multi-cannon while Segnen Exchange pursued an Advanced Dumbfire Launcher. The conflict demonstrated how alien archaeology had become a driver of corporate warfare, with the ghosts of an extinct civilization fueling contemporary human conflicts.

## Modern Relevance
Guardian technology remains a critical component of anti-xeno defense. The ancient war between Guardians and Thargoids provides essential context for understanding current Thargoid behavior—particularly their apparent hostility toward Guardian-derived technology. The corporate scramble for Guardian resources also established precedents for how commercial interests exploit xenoarchaeological discoveries, raising ethical questions about whether extinct civilizations' legacy should be commodified.
