## Biography

By March 3303, HR 1185 had become one of several Pleiades-adjacent systems where unprovoked Thargoid attacks shifted from isolated incidents to a coordinated offensive. Imperial and civilian vessels alike found themselves targeted by alien vessels without warning, shattering the tentative assumption that Thargoids might restrict hostilities to military targets. The Anti Xeno Initiative, which controlled the sparsely populated extraction system, found its defensive resources overwhelmed by the scale of the incursion.

Aegis later identified HR 1185 as a priority location for defensive installation construction, joining HIP 17225, HIP 17692 and other threatened systems in a network of fortified positions designed to slow the Thargoid advance. The system's anomalous position — governed by a patronage model under an anti-xeno militia rather than a conventional faction — made it both vulnerable and uniquely motivated to resist. HR 1185's experience proved that the Thargoid threat respected no borders, attacking systems regardless of their allegiance or military posture.
