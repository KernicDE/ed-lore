The Caspian Explorer represents Zorgon Peterson's entry into the specialised exobiology vessel market, designed to support the scientific study of alien lifeforms across diverse planetary environments. During extensive field trials in late 3311 and early 3312, the ship exceeded all internal performance projections, with independent pilots including CMDR BitNick contributing substantial quantities of exobiological data that validated the design's research capabilities. The vessel integrated advanced sampling equipment, specialised laboratories, and habitat modules optimised for extended operations in remote systems.

Zorgon Peterson confirmed full production of the Caspian Explorer in early 3312, making it available to the independent pilot community that had proven essential to its development through participation in trial programmes. The ship's success demonstrated the growing commercial viability of scientific exploration as a profession distinct from either pure cartography or resource extraction. Through the Caspian Explorer, Zorgon Peterson established a presence in a niche that bridged industrial manufacturing with the expanding frontiers of xenobiological research.
