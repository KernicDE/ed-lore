## Biography

In April 3311, Gutamaya Shipyards broke with decades of tradition by releasing the Corsair — the first commercially available multirole Imperial vessel that required no Navy rank qualification for purchase. Lead engineer Ines Allende declared the ship a 'significant milestone,' combining Gutamaya's characteristic elegant design with balanced hardpoint configuration, substantial cargo capacity, powerful thrusters, and four utility slots. The vessel's medium-size classification made it viable for trading with outposts inaccessible to larger Imperial ships, opening the manufacturer's market beyond military and aristocratic clientele.

Princess Aisling Duval's presence at the Capitol launch event provided the celebrity endorsement that Gutamaya clearly sought, with the Princess announcing she would add a Corsair to her personal fleet and praising the ship as proof of 'the Empire's commitment to innovation.' The release marked a subtle but significant shift in Imperial industrial policy: for the first time, one of the Empire's premier shipbuilders acknowledged that prestige and military exclusivity might be less profitable than selling versatile vessels to the broader pilot market.
