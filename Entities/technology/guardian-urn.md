Guardian urns constitute a category of artefacts recovered from archaeological sites associated with the ancient Guardian civilisation, which flourished millions of years before human spaceflight before disappearing under mysterious circumstances. These vessels, typically found alongside other Guardian relics such as orbs, totems, and tablets, represent tangible connections to a species that achieved remarkable technological and cultural sophistication. Archaeological analysis suggests the urns may have served ceremonial, funerary, or commemorative functions, though their precise purpose within Guardian society remains subject to ongoing research and debate.

During mid-3307, Aegis and Taurus Mining Ventures launched competing collection campaigns focused on Guardian artefacts, including urns, from sites in the PMD2009 48 system. Aegis specifically sought these objects to support its anti-xeno research programme, hoping that understanding Guardian technology would yield insights applicable to the Thargoid threat. The rival collection efforts highlighted both the scientific value of Guardian materials and the intense competition among human organisations to unlock their secrets, as conventional approaches to the alien conflict proved increasingly inadequate.
