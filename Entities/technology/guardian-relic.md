Guardian relics comprise a broad category of objects recovered from archaeological sites left by the ancient Guardian civilisation, which achieved remarkable technological and cultural sophistication before its mysterious disappearance millions of years ago. These artefacts, which include orbs, urns, totems, caskets, and tablets, vary widely in form and apparent function, offering tantalising glimpses into a species that developed interstellar capability long before humanity. Their study has driven major research programmes across scientific, military, and corporate institutions seeking to understand both Guardian history and the practical applications of their technology.

During mid-3307, Aegis and Taurus Mining Ventures launched competing campaigns to collect Guardian relics from sites in the PMD2009 48 system. Aegis specifically sought these objects to support a new anti-xeno research programme aimed at developing countermeasures against Thargoid aggression. The rival collection efforts highlighted both the scientific value of Guardian materials and the intense competition among human organisations to unlock their secrets, as conventional approaches to the alien threat proved increasingly inadequate and the pressure for breakthrough solutions mounted.
