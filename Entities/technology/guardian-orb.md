Guardian orbs constitute a distinctive category of artefacts recovered from sites associated with the ancient Guardian civilisation, which attained remarkable technological sophistication before its mysterious disappearance millions of years ago. These spherical objects, typically found alongside other Guardian relics such as totems, caskets, and tablets, represent some of the most visually striking remnants of a species that developed parallel to humanity but reached interstellar capability long before Earthly civilisations emerged. Archaeological analysis suggests the orbs may have served ceremonial, computational, or energy-storage functions, though their precise purpose remains subject to ongoing research.

During mid-3307, Aegis and Taurus Mining Ventures launched competing collection campaigns focused on Guardian artefacts, including orbs, from archaeological sites in the PMD2009 48 system. Aegis specifically sought these objects to support its anti-xeno research programme, hoping that understanding Guardian technology would yield insights applicable to the Thargoid threat. The rival collection efforts highlighted both the scientific value of Guardian materials and the intense competition among human organisations to unlock their secrets, as conventional approaches to the alien conflict proved increasingly inadequate.
