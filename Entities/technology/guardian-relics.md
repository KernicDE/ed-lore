Guardian relics comprise a broad category of objects recovered from archaeological sites associated with the ancient Guardian civilisation, which attained remarkable technological and cultural sophistication before its mysterious disappearance millions of years ago. These artefacts vary widely in form and apparent function, ranging from ceremonial objects to components that may have served practical technological purposes. Their study has provided humanity with crucial insights into a species that developed parallel to human evolution but reached interstellar capability long before Earthly civilisations emerged.

In early 3310, Aegis launched a major initiative to collect Guardian relics alongside unclassified relics for Ram Tah's classified anti-Titan research programme. Alba Tesreau expressed encouragement after witnessing laboratory tests, while Ishmael Palin joined the effort and Admiral Tanner cautioned against premature optimism regarding the proposed weapon's capabilities. The campaign's success in boosting Ram Tah's research demonstrated both the continued scientific potential of Guardian materials and the high stakes involved in adapting ancient technology to address contemporary existential threats.
