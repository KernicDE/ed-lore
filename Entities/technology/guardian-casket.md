Guardian caskets constitute a category of artefacts recovered from sites left by the ancient Guardian civilisation, which flourished millions of years before human spaceflight before disappearing under mysterious circumstances. These containers, along with totems, relics, and other objects, represent tangible connections to a species that achieved remarkable technological and cultural sophistication. Archaeological analysis suggests the caskets served ceremonial or commemorative functions, though their precise purpose within Guardian society remains subject to ongoing research and debate.

During mid-3307, Aegis and Taurus Mining Ventures launched competing collection campaigns focused on Guardian artefacts, including caskets, from sites in the PMD2009 48 system. Aegis sought these objects specifically to support anti-xeno research programmes aimed at developing countermeasures against Thargoid aggression. The rival efforts highlighted both the scientific significance of Guardian materials and the intense competition among human organisations to unlock their secrets, as the galaxy confronted an alien threat that conventional technology struggled to overcome.
