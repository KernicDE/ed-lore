## Biography

The Imperial Navy traces its origins to the Battle of Achenar in 2325, when forces led by Henson Duval annihilated a Federal expeditionary fleet sent to force the Achenar colony into the Federation. This victory not only secured the Empire's independence but also established the Navy's reputation as a formidable fighting force. The Navy's flagship vessel, the Majestic-class Interdictor, is both a warship and a symbol of Imperial grandeur — senators and high-ranking officers command these vessels as mobile palaces and military headquarters.

The Navy's structure reflects the Empire's feudal character. While a central command reports to the Emperor and the Imperial Senate, many naval forces are effectively private armies raised by individual senators. Denton Patreus, for example, commands a fleet of Majestic-class Interdictors and extensive ground forces loyal to his house. This decentralisation provides the Empire with enormous military flexibility but also creates tensions between central and provincial forces.

In the 34th century, the Imperial Navy has fought numerous campaigns. In 3301, it supported Senator Patreus's war against Emperor's Dawn, the hardline insurgent group that had assassinated Emperor Hengist Duval. In 3304–3305, it defeated Nova Imperium, a separatist movement that challenged Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval's legitimacy by promoting Hadrian Augustus Duval as an alternative claimant. The Navy has also conducted operations against the Kumo Crew pirate syndicate in the Pegasi Sector and contributed forces to anti-Thargoid operations, both through Aegis and independently after Aegis's dissolution.

The Navy's relationship with the Imperial Family is intimate and sometimes tragic. Emperor Hengist Duval died en route to the Imperial Navy Medical Facility near the Imperial Palace after being stabbed at his wedding in 3301. The facility, like much of the Navy's infrastructure on Capitol, is located within the shadow of the Imperial Palace, reflecting the interwoven nature of military and royal authority.

The Imperial Navy's rivalry with the Federal Navy is centuries old and deeply ingrained. While both forces have cooperated against the Thargoids, their competition for prestige, territory, and resources continues through proxy conflicts and peacetime posturing. The Navy's officer corps is drawn from the Imperial aristocracy and its client families, creating a culture of honour, tradition, and fierce loyalty to the Emperor — values that distinguish it from the more corporate and careerist Federal Navy.
