## Biography

The Imperial Navy traces its origins to the Battle of Achenar in 2325, when Henson Duval's forces annihilated a Federal expeditionary fleet, securing the Empire's independence. Its flagship, the Majestic-class Interdictor, serves as both warship and symbol of Imperial grandeur — senators command these vessels as mobile palaces and military headquarters.

The Navy's structure reflects the Empire's feudal character. While a central command reports to the Emperor and Senate, many forces are effectively private armies raised by individual senators. This decentralisation provides military flexibility but creates tensions between central and provincial forces. In the 34th century, the Navy has fought campaigns against Emperor's Dawn (3301), Nova Imperium (3304-3305), the Kumo Crew, and contributed to anti-Thargoid operations.

The Navy's relationship with the Imperial Family is intimate and sometimes tragic — Emperor Hengist Duval died en route to the Imperial Navy Medical Facility after his assassination in 3301. Its rivalry with the Federal Navy is centuries old, continuing through proxy conflicts even as both forces cooperate against the Thargoids.
