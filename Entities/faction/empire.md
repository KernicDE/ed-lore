## Biography

### Founding and the Duval Dynasty

The Empire began as an idealistic project. In 2292, Marlin Duval — a wealthy, Earth-born anti-Federation activist — led an expedition beyond Federal reach to the Achenar system. On Achenar 6d, a moon orbiting a gas giant that would become known as Capitol, she founded the Republic of Achenar. Her vision was of an egalitarian society free from the stifling corporate control of the Federation, governed by an administrative council with representatives managing different aspects of the colony.

That vision died with her. In 2296, Marlin and her immediate family perished in an apparent speeder crash. Her brother Henson Duval, who had never shared her democratic ideals, seized control of the Republic's senate, disbanded the ruling council, and appointed himself sole leader for life. He capitalised on the colonists' anti-Federation sentiment to advocate jingoism and authoritarianism. In 2320, Henson declared himself the first Emperor, and the Republic's transformation into the Empire was complete.

The Duval dynasty has ruled ever since. Female children in the family were reportedly prevented by genetic techniques — a manifestation of the vehement hatred for Marlin's principles. The current Emperor, Arissa Lavigny-Duval, is the 16th ruler to sit on the Imperial Throne, elected by the Senate in 3301 following the assassination of her father Hengist Duval.

### The Battle of Achenar

The Empire's independence was forged in war. As Achenar thrived, the Federation repeatedly attempted to bring the colony into the Federal Accord. Each overture was refused. Tensions came to a head when reports emerged that terraforming efforts on Achenar 6d had resulted in the extinction of the planet's native intelligent species, the mudlarks. The Federation asserted that Henson Duval had deliberately ordered the moon's sterilisation; the Empire maintained that the mudlarks were tragic victims of bacteria introduced accidentally by colonists.

Regardless of the truth, the Federation used the incident as a pretext. In late 2323, Admiral Richard Morgan assembled an invasion fleet at Beta Hydri and began the transit to Achenar. The region was relatively unexplored, and the fleet lacked adequate logistical support. Morgan chose to engage in a single large battle — a fatal error. Achenar was alerted and laid an ambush, using pack-hunting formations to harass the Federal fleet before targeting its supply lines.

For the remainder of 2324, Morgan failed to establish a foothold while his fleet dwindled from attrition. In 2325, Emperor Henson pressed every available civilian and military ship into service, creating a "People's Fleet" for the counterattack remembered as the "Great Battle of Liberation." Morgan was driven into retreat, his ships harried all the way back to Beta Hydri. Skirmishing continued sporadically until 2379, when Sirius Corporation began mediating negotiations. The treaty of 2381 granted Achenar full autonomy and official recognition of the hereditary imperial title.

Henson ordered the construction of the Hall of Martyrs beneath the Imperial Palace on Capitol, commemorating the 16,032 citizens who died. In the Federation, Morgan's defeat triggered a crisis of confidence in Earth-based leadership, contributing to the decision to move the Federal capital from Washington, D.C. to Olympus Village on Mars.

### Society and Government

The Empire is based on a "cliens" system modeled on ancient Rome. Society is strictly stratified, with movement between levels determined by wealth, patronage, and influence. At the apex sits the Emperor, below whom the Imperial Senate of 1,000 Senators wields legislative, judicial, and electoral power. The hierarchy extends downward through Patrons, Clients, and finally Citizens — every Imperial Citizen has a right to representation, however indirect.

Status and honour are the Empire's twin currencies. It is acceptable to flaunt wealth, but treating people well is a question of honour — a principle that extends even to slaves. Having an unpaid debt is considered utterly dishonourable; an honourable Imperial citizen would sell themselves into slavery to clear a debt they could not otherwise repay. Imperial slavery is legal, culturally entrenched, and regulated. Some Senators regularly speak out against it, but from the basis of honour rather than advocating for its abolition.

Senators themselves are above the law. They can order executions and even kill with their own hands, though they may rarely be held to account by the Emperor. Very little is illegal in the Empire, though many things are frowned upon, such as excessive narcotics use. This creates a society where power is nakedly displayed and personal honour is the primary constraint on behaviour.

### The Thargoid Crisis and Superpower Rivalry

The Empire's relationship with the Federation has been defined by cold war for nearly a millennium. The two superpowers constantly compete for territory and resources, most notably in the Alioth system and, more recently, the Pleiades Nebula.

In 3302, the discovery of barnacles and meta-alloys in the Pleiades triggered a new phase of confrontation. Senator Zemina Torval condemned the Federation's deployment of warships in Merope as an attempt to claim possession of the barnacles and their valuable meta-alloys. The cold war over Pleiades resources continued until the Thargoid threat forced a change.

In August 3303, the Empire joined the Federation and Alliance in establishing Aegis, a joint superpower initiative to coordinate Thargoid research. Professor Alba Tesreau of the Achenar Research Council announced the organisation, noting that while there was "antagonism between our governments, there is a strong desire for collaboration and cooperation within the scientific community." The cold war thawed temporarily, though commentators like Daxton Sung of the Imperial Herald argued that neither superpower had incentive to formally end the conflict.

By late 3303, both the Empire and Federation had withdrawn from the Pleiades, leaving Aegis to manage anti-xeno operations. The Empire later committed to Salvation's anti-Thargoid campaign before returning to independent research after the HIP 22460 catastrophe.

### The NMLA Crisis and Internal Threats

The Empire faced its most serious internal threat in centuries during the NMLA (Neo-Marlinist Liberation Army) crisis. The NMLA, which advocated democratic representation and drew on the republican ideals of Marlin Duval's original vision, launched a campaign of starport bombings across all three superpowers. In September 3306, following the murder of Prince Harold Duval, Senator Denton Patreus mobilised Imperial military forces and declared martial law in systems where Marlinism was popular.

The crisis exposed deep fractures within the Imperial establishment. In January 3308, ACT (Affiliated Counter-Terrorism unit) revealed that the Lords of Restoration — a secret cabal of Senators — had orchestrated Emperor Arissa's abduction in 3307, using their position and corrupt Imperial Intelligence agents to maintain the deception for over a year. The Senate had blocked ACT's investigation into links between Imperial Intelligence and the NMLA, with Senator Karl Nerva calling for ACT to be disbanded while Senator Caspian Leopold argued for rigorous examination of the allegations.

The scandal represented one of the most profound breaches of trust in Imperial history: the very Senators charged with protecting the Emperor had conspired to abduct her.

### Succession and the Future

In October 3309, Senator Lysandra Courcelle raised concerns that Emperor Arissa had not produced any direct heirs after eight years on the throne — an unprecedented situation in a dynasty where "every Emperor of the last thousand years has had one or more children." Chancellor Anders Blaine defended the Emperor, but the question of succession remains unresolved. Princess Aisling Duval and Hadrian Duval — with his son Astrid — represent alternative branches of the bloodline, but the continuity of the direct Duval line is uncertain.

The Empire today remains what it has been for nearly a thousand years: a society of stark contrasts, where honour and brutality coexist, where slavery is both a shame and a virtue, and where the glittering Imperial Palace on Capitol stands as a monument to both Marlin Duval's broken dream and Henson Duval's ruthless ambition.
