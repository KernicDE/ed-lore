Universal Liberty operated as an independent charitable organisation dedicated to the abolition of slavery throughout Imperial space. Founded by individuals with direct experience of bondage, including Jarl Toredo who was born into slavery, the group advocated for legal reform through public campaigns and political pressure. Their work challenged one of the Empire's most entrenched institutions, making them both celebrated among reformers and controversial among traditionalists who viewed Imperial Slavery as an essential economic and social pillar.

In mid-3304, Universal Liberty merged with Princess Aisling Duval's personal anti-slavery initiative to form Unchain, a unified movement combining royal patronage with grassroots activism. Toredo announced the union publicly, calling for the complete abolition of legal slavery across the Empire. This collaboration demonstrated how independent charities could amplify their influence by aligning with sympathetic figures within the Imperial establishment, while also highlighting the growing tensions between progressive and conservative elements of Imperial society.
