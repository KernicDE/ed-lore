## Biography

A heavy construction contractor with no exclusive superpower allegiance, Wreaken Construction has built a reputation for secrecy as much as for engineering. In November 3302, the company assembled a large convoy of construction ships and support vessels in the Zearla system, refusing to disclose its purpose while maintaining heavy patrols and warning off approaching vessels. The operation drove Wreaken shares up five per cent and fuelled speculation that the convoy was linked to Admiral Denton Patreus's controversial comments concerning the London Treaty.

Wreaken's security culture proved lethal in January 3303, when an unidentified individual was found dead from radiation exposure at a company site in the Col 70 sector — a death the corporation dismissed as the victim's own fault for breaching secured facilities. By 3307, Wreaken had expanded into the LTT 198 system, where it established an industrial presence in direct competition with Senator Zemina Torval's newly formed Torval-Mastopolos Mining, reigniting a long-standing corporate rivalry.
