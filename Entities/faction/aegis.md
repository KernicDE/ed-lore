## Biography

Aegis was established in August 3303 as humanity's first organised response to the resurgent Thargoid threat, uniting scientists and military personnel from the Alliance, Empire, and Federation under a single command structure. Professor Alba Tesreau of the Achenar Research Council directed scientific operations, while Admiral Aden Tanner of the Federal Navy coordinated military affairs. The organisation maintained three primary divisions: Aegis Research, focused on Thargoid and Guardian xenology; Aegis Defence, operating anti-xeno task forces; and the Eagle Eye surveillance network, monitoring Thargoid incursion patterns.

In its early years, Aegis achieved significant progress. Scientists mapped Thargoid barnacle sites, developed the first effective anti-xeno missiles and shutdown field neutralisers, and established forward laboratories at The Oracle in Delphi and other Pleiades outposts. The organisation's research into meta-alloys — organic compounds produced by Thargoid barnacles — proved crucial for developing countermeasures against Thargoid caustic weaponry and for repairing stations damaged in Thargoid attacks.

The turning point came in 3307. Aegis deployed the megaship Alexandria to Delphi in April to collect Guardian artefacts for research. The vessel's cargo attracted Thargoid vessels, forcing it to flee through multiple systems. On 3 June 3307, the Alexandria jumped from HIP 16538 toward HIP 30944 but never arrived. Admiral Tanner declared the vessel missing with all 4,000 crew presumed dead, likely hyperdicted by Thargoid forces. The loss shattered public confidence in Aegis and prompted the superpowers to order a partial suspension and independent inquiry chaired by Dr. Paul Baumann.

The Baumann Report, completed in January 3308, recommended Aegis's complete dissolution. The report cited organisational complacency, failure to develop new generations of AX weaponry, and an over-reliance on independent pilots to bear the combat burden. Professor Tesreau, Director Yazhu Xing, and Dr. Mia Valencourt were forced to resign. Chief Administrator Carter Armstrong, who had become de facto leader, argued for reinstatement after the Alexandria's wreckage was discovered in Wregoe TC-X b29-0 in January 3308 and logs revealed the ship had been sabotaged by Azimuth Biochemicals operatives rather than lost to Thargoid attack. The superpowers rejected this appeal, and Aegis was formally dismantled, its research distributed to national academies and its military functions devolved to the Federal Navy, Imperial Navy, Alliance Defence Force, and Sirius Navy.

Aegis's legacy remains contested. Critics view it as a cautionary tale of bureaucratic failure; supporters note that it pioneered the scientific study of Thargoid biology and produced technologies that saved countless lives during the Second Thargoid War. Its collapse also created the vacuum that Salvation exploited, fundamentally altering humanity's approach to the xeno threat.
