## Biography

The Federal Intelligence Agency (FIA) is the Federation's principal domestic intelligence and security service, responsible for counter-terrorism, counter-intelligence, and internal security operations. Though less publicly visible than the Federal Navy, the FIA has played a central role in several major crises across the 3300s.

### Early Operations (3301)

The FIA first appeared in public records on 19 July 3301, when agents conducted a surprise raid to arrest Federal Governor Joel Xander of BD-22 3573. Xander and his family were escorted off-planet in an FIA-marked dropship, signalling the agency's willingness to move against corrupt Federal officials with close ties to local power structures.

### The League of Reparation (3304)

In 3304, the FIA joined a tri-superpower taskforce alongside the Imperial Internal Security Service (IISS) and Alliance Interpol to investigate the League of Reparation, a terrorist organisation murdering descendants of INRA personnel. Senior Agent Tanya Ramirez served as the FIA's representative on the taskforce.

The League's campaign began with the assassination of Imperial Senator Nestor Cartesius and expanded to include multiple murders across Federal, Imperial, and Alliance space. Victims were killed with lethal nerve toxin or military-grade ship weapons, their vessels laser-branded "FOR JAMESON" in reference to Commander John Jameson, the INRA pilot who deployed the mycoid weapon against the Thargoids.

The taskforce's investigation led to the arrest of Alliance Admiral George Varma at Donaldson starport in Alioth on 13 July 3304 — an operation conducted by Captain Niamh Seutonia (IISS) and Senior Agent Ramirez that caused significant diplomatic friction. Varma was later exonerated when Deputy Inspector Mara Klatt revealed that Rear Admiral Riri McAllister was the true mastermind, known as "Nexus." McAllister was arrested and charged with terrorism; by 28 July 3304, the taskforce confirmed the League had been fully dismantled.

### The Far God Cult Investigation (3304)

In August 3304, the FIA launched a sweeping investigation into the Far God cult, a religious sect that worshipped the Thargoids as divine beings. Senior Agent Micah Whitefield led the operation, stating the need to confirm whether cult members were in communication with Thargoid forces.

Armed Federal troops raided hive-chapels across multiple systems, dragging cult members into armoured vehicles. The arrests proved deeply controversial: some citizens shielded worshippers while others, led by anti-cult campaigner Juanita Bishop, cheered the crackdown. The FIA also arrested Dr Alfred Ulyanov, a leading exotheologian researching the cult, seizing his research materials — an act that prompted Professor Siobhan Vogel of the Interstellar Association of Universities to file a formal complaint calling it a "dangerous precedent."

The investigation concluded on 29 September 3304, when Whitefield announced that information provided by journalist Gethin Okonkwo had been verified as genuine and that no evidence of Thargoid communication existed. All detained worshippers were released. The episode became a notable example of civil liberties tensions during the Thargoid crisis, though some cult members had vanished during the raids and were never accounted for.

### The Lucifer Device and Children of Tothos (3305)

In February 3305, the FIA investigated the discovery of an unregistered research outpost where all occupants had been murdered. Senior Agent Rochelle Karim revealed that the outpost was conducting advanced nuclear research and that a portable 300-megaton fusion warhead — codenamed the Lucifer Device — had been stolen.

The investigation traced the theft to the Children of Tothos, a fringe cult led by Barnabas Cole. FIA interrogations revealed that corrupt Zlota Federal Holdings executive Willard Morgenstern had been secretly funding the weapons research and, while intoxicated, disclosed the outpost's location to the cult. On 1 March 3305, the FIA confirmed the cult possessed the Lucifer Device and launched an interstellar manhunt.

The crisis escalated on 14 March 3305 when the Children of Tothos seized Archambault Terminal in the Chun Tstar system, threatening to detonate the weapon with millions of civilians trapped inside. The FIA opted for negotiation over assault, given the catastrophic potential of a frontal attack.

### Nature and Methods

The FIA operates as a Federal law enforcement and intelligence agency with broad authority to investigate threats to the Federation's internal security. Its agents have been known to operate in foreign jurisdictions — sometimes without local approval — as demonstrated during the Varma arrest in Alliance space. The agency has shown both cooperative instincts (working with IISS and Alliance Interpol) and controversial overreach (the Ulyanov arrest, mass cult detentions).

Notable senior agents include Tanya Ramirez (League of Reparation taskforce), Micah Whitefield (Far God cult investigation), and Rochelle Karim (Lucifer Device case).
