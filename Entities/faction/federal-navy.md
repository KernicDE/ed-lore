## Biography

The Federal Navy traces its origins to the military forces that unified during the Federation's founding in 2242. Over the centuries it grew into one of the largest naval forces in human space, though it suffered a historic humiliation at the Battle of Achenar in 2325, where an expeditionary fleet sent to force the Achenar colony into the Federation was annihilated by the colonists led by Henson Duval. This defeat not only ensured the Empire's independence but also prompted the Federation to relocate its capital from Earth to Mars.

By the 34th century, the Federal Navy's pinnacle vessel is the Farragut Battle Cruiser, a two-kilometre-long capital ship capable of carrying multiple Federal Corvettes and fighter squadrons. The deployment of a Farragut is generally sufficient to end any but the largest military engagements. The Navy also operates extensive ground forces and maintains bases across Federal territory.

The Navy's relationship with civilian government has been fraught. During the Lugh campaign in 3301, President Jasmina Halsey ordered Federal pilots to attack an unarmed refugee convoy, killing an estimated 9,322 civilians. This atrocity caused many senior naval commanders to lose faith in Halsey and transfer their loyalty to Shadow President Zachary Hudson. The subsequent Vote of No Confidence that installed Hudson was supported by key naval figures — most notably Fleet Admiral Lucas Vincent, who was later revealed to have conspired with Core Dynamics CEO Jupiter Rochester to destroy Starship One and assassinate Halsey in 3301. Vincent's trial in 3307 exposed the plot; he was sentenced to life imprisonment, and Rochester was jailed for life after his breakaway Jupiter Division was defeated in battle.

During the Thargoid conflicts, the Federal Navy contributed forces to Aegis and later operated its own independent anti-xeno task forces after Aegis's dissolution in 3308. The Navy's traditional rivalry with the Imperial Navy occasionally complicated joint operations, but both services recognised the existential nature of the Thargoid threat. The Federal Navy remains the dominant military power in Federal space, though its reputation was significantly damaged by the Starship One conspiracy and its close association with the Hudson administration's controversial policies.
