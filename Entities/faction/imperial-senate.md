## Description

### Structure and Composition

The Imperial Senate consists of 1,000 Senators who answer only to the Emperor. Sessions are held in Senate House, located near the Imperial Palace on Capitol in the Achenar system. While the chamber physically exists, most Senators attend sessions virtually, reflecting the Empire's vast territorial scale.

The Senate is routinely presided over by the Chancellor — currently Anders Blaine, who represents Facece. The Emperor attends only on certain occasions. When both the Emperor and Chancellor are absent, the Senate is run by one Senator who assumes the role of Leader of the House, a position currently held by Zemina Torval of Synteini.

Senators represent a hierarchical chain of patronage: Senators → Patrons → Clients → Citizens. Every Imperial Citizen has a right to representation by a Client, every Client by a Patron, and every Patron by a Senator. Senators generally represent power bases that can span many star systems or specific interest groups, and typically maintain hundreds of Patrons — though the exact number is at each Senator's discretion.

The Senate is structurally flat by law, but in practice operates through power blocks led by the most influential Senators. A Senator's voting power is determined by the total number of citizens they currently represent. The most powerful Senators can wield a hundred times the voting power of others, and a Senate majority can be achieved by just a few of the major Senators. Honour demands that all Senators' views be listened to politely before voting against them.

### Powers and Functions

The Senate possesses three core powers: legislative, judicial, and electoral.

**Legislative:** Senators regularly discuss and vote on important issues of the Empire and enforce the Emperor's laws. They alone have the authority to change Imperial law. All Senators and the Emperor can sit in the Imperial Senate, though it is rare for all to participate simultaneously.

**Financial:** The Senate enjoys access to the Imperial treasury, allowing it to fund major commissions and programmes. This financial control makes it one of the most powerful institutions in the Empire.

**Electoral:** The legitimacy of an Emperor depends on Senate recognition. When an Emperor dies without a clear line of succession, the Senate must elect a suitable candidate within one month. The rules are ancient and strict: the candidate must be of royal blood or of royal marriage with the House of Duval, and must secure a clear majority backing in the final count.

**Senatorial Protection:** A majority of Senators can agree to recognise an individual as a ward of the Senate, conferring the highest level of personal security the Empire can offer. Protected individuals are transported to undisclosed, highly secure locations known only to certain Senators. Known recipients include Hadrian Augustus Duval, Lady Astrid Minerva-Duval, and Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval herself.

### The 3301 Imperial Succession

The Senate's electoral power was most dramatically demonstrated following the assassination of Emperor Hengist Duval on 22 August 3301. Chancellor Anders Blaine opened the succession debate on 7 September, stating that the law required a decision by 5 October.

Blaine urged Senators to consider Hengist's wishes, noting the Emperor had intended for Senator Arissa Lavigny-Duval to become heir. The candidates were required to be of royal blood or royal marriage with a clear Senate majority. The race quickly narrowed to three main contenders: Chancellor Blaine himself, Princess Aisling Duval, and Senator Arissa Lavigny-Duval.

Blaine's candidacy collapsed when connections between his advisors and the Emperor's Dawn insurgent group were revealed. Aisling Duval's chances were similarly damaged by her own advisors' ties to the same organisation. With Senator Lavigny-Duval receiving public support from Senators Denton Patreus and Zemina Torval, she emerged as the leading contender. On 5 October 3301, the Senate elected Arissa Lavigny-Duval as the 16th Emperor of the Empire.

### The NMLA Crisis and Marlinist Debate

The Senate played a central role in the Empire's response to the Neo-Marlinist Liberation Army (NMLA) terrorist campaign. In September 3306, following the murder of Prince Harold Duval and starport bombings, Senator Denton Patreus mobilised Imperial military forces and declared martial law in Ackwada, Beatis, Chana, and Nahuatl — systems where Marlinist republican ideals were popular. Addressing the Senate, Patreus vowed: "There will be justice for the murder of Prince Harold Duval and justice for those citizens who perished in flames. The Marlinists will pay dearly for these atrocities!"

By June 3307, the Senate was divided over whether to pursue diplomacy with the Marlinist Colonies. Senator Lorcan Scordato proposed formal diplomatic relations after a fact-finding mission, arguing that the Marlinist community sought peaceful co-existence. Other Senators, led by Patreus, rejected negotiations, stating: "As a matter of honour and principle, the Empire does not negotiate with terrorists."

### The Lords of Restoration Scandal

In January 3308, the Senate's own protective powers were turned against it. Captain Saskia Landau of the Affiliated Counter-Terrorism unit (ACT) publicly stated that the NMLA's dark-comms network was closely related to the Omega Grid system used exclusively by Imperial Intelligence. She accused rogue elements within Imperial Intelligence of training and equipping the NMLA.

When Landau called for a dedicated investigation, the Senate blocked it. Senator Karl Nerva argued that ACT should be disbanded, while Senator Caspian Leopold countered: "To alleviate any lack of confidence in our security services, we should examine Captain Landau's allegation rigorously."

The obstruction was later revealed to be part of a deeper conspiracy. The Lords of Restoration — a secret cabal of Senators — had orchestrated Emperor Arissa's abduction in January 3307 while she was travelling to a senatorial protection site. They maintained the deception until February 3308, using their position as Senators and corrupt Imperial Intelligence agents to manipulate the Senate and the Empire. The scandal represented one of the most profound breaches of trust in the Senate's thousand-year history.

### The Succession Question of 3309

In October 3309, Senator Lysandra Courcelle raised concerns that threatened to destabilise the Imperial establishment. She formally questioned why Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval had not produced any direct heirs after eight years on the throne:

"The continuation of the Duval bloodline has formed the linchpin of our society for over a thousand years. But it has been eight years since Arissa Lavigny-Duval became our ruler. In that time there has been no spouse or consort, no indications of marriage, and no biological heir to the throne. We politely remind the Emperor of her duty to ensure the line of succession."

Chancellor Blaine defended the Emperor, stating: "Her Majesty has sacrificed much for the Empire. It is not our place to question her personal choices, but rather to support her vision for our prosperity and security."

The debate highlighted a constitutional tension: while the Senate had elected Arissa, it could not compel her to produce heirs. Princess Aisling Duval's romantic liaisons and Hadrian Duval's son Astrid represented alternative branches of the bloodline, but the question of Arissa's own succession remained unresolved.

### Relationship with the Emperor

The balance of power between the Senate and the Emperor has shifted throughout Imperial history. During the long illness of Emperor Hesketh Duval, the Senate grew in power; his son Hengist Duval worked to reclaim imperial authority after taking the throne. If the Emperor is unable and wills it, the Chancellor can rule in the Emperor's stead.

The Senate's role is fundamentally conservative: it exists to maintain the stability of the Empire and the Duval dynasty. Yet it is also the body through which challengers to imperial authority must operate — whether through the election of a new Emperor, the blocking of investigations, or the declaration of martial law against internal enemies. The tension between these roles — stabiliser and potential usurper — defines the Senate's place in Imperial politics.
