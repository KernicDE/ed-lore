## Biography

The Federation was formed in 2242, unifying Earth and its off-world colonies under a single government after the Third World War and the subsequent rise of the United States of the Americas as the dominant global power. Its constitution and laws were derived from pre-war governments but significantly simplified. The Federation expanded rapidly into interstellar space, establishing colonies in systems such as Tau Ceti, Delta Pavonis, Altair, and Beta Hydri.

The Federation's defining characteristic is its corporate nature. Power is distributed among member states, but real influence rests with mega-corporations — Core Dynamics, Lakon Spaceways, Faulcon DeLacy, Saud Kruger, and others — that control the economic and political landscape. Employees live under corporate patronage, with their votes guided by employer interests. Corruption, bribery, and underhanded influence are endemic. Individual freedoms are nominally protected but frequently subordinated to profit; the society is relentlessly consumerist, with "throwaway" culture driving constant demand for new technologies and luxuries.

The Federation's political system is presidential, with elections held every four years followed by a mandatory Vote of No Confidence. The two dominant parties are the Republicans, who advocate for military strength, corporate deregulation, and minimal government; and the Liberals, who support social programmes, diplomatic engagement, and reduced military spending. Presidents serve extended terms if they survive the confidence vote, as Zachary Hudson did in 3305.

The Federation suffered its greatest historical trauma at the Battle of Achenar in 2325. An expeditionary fleet sent to force the independent Achenar colony into membership was annihilated by forces led by Henson Duval. This defeat not only secured the Empire's independence but also forced the Federation to relocate its capital from Earth to Mars to appease discontented member systems. Sporadic warfare between the Federation and Empire continued for decades until a treaty was signed in the 2380s.

In the 34th century, the Federation remains the most populous superpower, though its influence is increasingly challenged by the Empire and Alliance. The Thargoid conflicts exposed weaknesses in its military-centric approach, while internal scandals — including the Starship One conspiracy and the Proactive Detection Bureau surveillance programme — have eroded public trust. The election of Felicia Winters in 3309 marked a potential shift toward Liberal governance, though the deeply entrenched corporate interests that define Federal politics remain as powerful as ever.
