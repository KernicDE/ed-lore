## Biography

The Imperial Court functions as the inner circle of Imperial governance, comprising nobles and officials loyal to the House of Duval who advise the Emperor and manage affairs of state. Following the foiled assassination attempt on Emperor Hengist Duval in April 3301, members of the Imperial Court were instrumental in the subsequent investigation, with Count von Auerburg, Earl Rednam and Earl Khaelmin dispatched to follow up on intelligence obtained from the interrogation of the captured attacker.

The Court's actions during the crisis underscored its role as both a political instrument and a security apparatus, with Princess Arissa Lavigny-Duval relying on its loyal members to coordinate efforts outside the traditional channels of the Imperial Senate. The investigation revealed the existence of a broader conspiracy against the Emperor, prompting Arissa to warn enemies of the Court that they would not receive a second chance to strike.
