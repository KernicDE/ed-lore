## Biography

The Federal Security Service (FSS) is the Federation's domestic intelligence agency, responsible for counter-terrorism, internal surveillance, and protecting Federal institutions from subversion. Operating under the authority of the President and Congress, the FSS has broad powers to monitor civilian communications, investigate threats, and coordinate with Federal law enforcement. Its jurisdiction covers all Federal systems, making it one of the most powerful security organisations in the galaxy.

The FSS gained notoriety during the Nine Martyrs crisis when it established the Proactive Detection Bureau, a mass surveillance programme that scans billions of civilian communications for terrorist activity. While the FSS defended the PDB as essential for national security, civil liberties groups and the Federal Times condemned it as unconstitutional overreach. The agency's close relationship with the Mars Tribune and other corporate media outlets has also raised concerns about the blurring of intelligence and propaganda in Federal politics.
