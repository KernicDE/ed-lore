## Biography

The Imperial Herald is the Empire's most influential news organisation, operating from its headquarters on Capitol in the Achenar system. Unlike the corporate-controlled media of the Federation, the Herald functions as an organ of state — its editorial line generally aligns with Imperial policy, but it also serves as a forum for the competing factions that characterise Imperial politics.

The Herald's coverage of the succession crisis of 3301 established its reputation for detailed political reporting. It published the definitive profile of Brendan Paul Darius, the assassin who killed Emperor Hengist Duval, tracing his upbringing in Eotienses as a client family of Senator Denton Patreus, his military service on Patreus's Majestic-class Interdictors, and his rise to patron status in the Senator's liaison team. The article's conclusion — "why would such a loyal man kill his Emperor?" — encapsulated the mystery that dominated Imperial politics for months.

The Herald maintains extensive bureaus across Imperial space, from Core Systems to frontier territories. Its reporters cover Imperial Senate sessions, military campaigns, court ceremonies, and the activities of the Imperial Family. During the Thargoid conflicts, it reported on both the official Aegis position and dissenting voices within the Empire, including Princess Aisling Duval's criticism of INRA's historical experiments on live Thargoids.

While the Herald is fundamentally supportive of the Imperial establishment, it has published critical investigations when they align with establishment interests. Its coverage of the NMLA threat emphasised the danger to Imperial citizens and the legitimacy of counter-terrorism measures, while its reporting on the Marlinist refugee crisis reflected the official position that Marlinists should be treated as potential security risks. The Herald's relationship with Princess Aisling Duval is particularly notable — it covers her activities extensively, reflecting both her popularity and the establishment's concern about her reformist agenda.

In the broader galactic media landscape, the Imperial Herald represents the Empire's perspective to the outside world. Its coverage of Federal and Alliance affairs is filtered through an Imperial lens, emphasising the stability and tradition of Imperial governance in contrast to what it portrays as the chaos of Federal democracy and the weakness of Alliance consensus politics.
