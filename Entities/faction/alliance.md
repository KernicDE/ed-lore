## Biography

### Origins on Alioth

The Alliance of Independent Systems was born from violence. Alioth — the system that would become its capital — was contested territory for centuries, fought over by the Federation and Empire in generation after generation of war. The system's inhabitants endured colonial exploitation, corporate price-fixing, and military occupation.

The turning point came in 3228, when Federal corporations supplying one of Alioth's main population centres attempted to raise prices. This triggered a full-scale revolt. Federal and Imperial forces were dispatched to crush the uprising, but the resistance — reinforced by fighters from neighbouring independent systems — successfully repelled both superpowers. The Empire withdrew first, unwilling to commit to a protracted campaign far from its centre of power. The Federation followed when it realised how unpopular the war had become among its own citizens.

In the wake of this victory, Mic Turner and his life-long partner Meredith Argent proposed a new political unit for those who wished to exist outside both the Federation and the Empire. In 3230, the Alliance of Independent Systems was formally established on Alioth. Its founding principle was simple: to protect and support independent systems while preserving their autonomy. Over the next two decades, more than twenty systems joined — some independent from the start, others revolting from Imperial and Federal control.

### Government and Power Structure

The Alliance is governed by the Alliance Assembly, a body of councillors from member systems who establish common policies and treaties. The notional head of government is the Alliance Prime Minister, elected by the Assembly for a six-year term. In practice, however, real power rests with the Council of Admirals, which commands the Alliance Defence Force (ADF).

The Alliance's complex structure of committees, power-sharing agreements, and system-level autonomy makes it less centralised than the Federation or Empire. This is both a strength — individual systems retain significant self-governance — and a weakness — decisive action can be slow and fractious.

Between 3304 and 3305, the Alliance experimented with a separate presidential office. President Gibson Kincaid demanded executive powers be transferred from the Prime Minister to the President. When the Assembly rejected this, Kincaid orchestrated a conspiracy to move the Alliance's political centre from Alioth to Zaonce, intending to establish himself as supreme ruler. The conspiracy was exposed, Kincaid was removed from office and charged with treason, and the Assembly abolished the presidency entirely in June 3305 to prevent similar abuses.

### Edmund Mahon

Edmund Mahon has shaped Alliance policy since 3301 and remains the only Prime Minister in Alliance history to serve a second term. Born in 3187 to a family of corn farmers on Birmingham in the Diso system — then a Federation-controlled world — Mahon demonstrated political cunning from an early age. He won a Federal scholarship to study law at Alioth University, but lost it after publicly criticising the Federal government on his personal video channel. He worked as a bartender to pay his tuition and eventually graduated in political science.

Mahon's political career began with a failed city council run against a corporate-backed candidate, but his debate performances built a significant supporter base. By age 27, he was working with Irene Mendel to break Diso away from the Federation and into the Alliance. When Diso succeeded in 3287, Mahon was already an accomplished politician. He was elected Prime Minister in early 3301.

Mahon's defining characteristic is diplomatic caution combined with strategic patience. When the Crimson State Group sought Alliance military support for Lugh's independence movement in March 3301, Mahon explicitly refused, stating that "no laws have been broken, no rights have been abused" and that the Alliance would not intervene in legitimate Federal governance. This decision was controversial — Lugh's leadership later declined to join the Alliance, possibly out of resentment — but it demonstrated Mahon's commitment to the rule of law over ideological sympathies.

### Economic and Corporate Policy

The Alliance relies heavily on corporate partnerships to achieve goals that other superpowers might pursue through centralised government programmes. Its terraforming programme, run in cooperation with Big G Corporation, applies proprietary "World Blending Technology" to transform barren worlds into habitable planets across multiple systems. The programme is funded through open contracts with independent pilots and corporations.

The Alliance-Sirius Corporation strategic defence pact, signed in January 3308, represents one of Mahon's most significant and controversial achievements. The pact grants the Alliance access to advanced Sirius technology and military support for anti-xeno operations. In exchange, Sirius Corporation was given priority access to financial opportunities in Alliance space and deployed four megaships to Alliance systems (Alioth, Arimpox, Di Jian, and Reorte). Admiral Nikolas Glass of the Sirius Navy was appointed to the Council of Admirals.

The pact was approved by a slim majority in a rare closed-door joint session of the Assembly and Council of Admirals. Many terms remain classified. Critics, led by Councillor Nakato Kaine of Tionisla, argue that the Alliance has become dangerously dependent on corporate interests and that inviting Sirius into Alliance space without full Assembly approval undermines democratic principles.

### Foreign Relations and the Thargoid Crisis

The Alliance has consistently pursued a diplomatic middle path between the Federation's militarism and the Empire's authoritarianism. During the Thargoid crisis, Mahon initially called for caution, arguing that it had not been proven that destroyed Federal ships were victims of Thargoid attacks and that much could be learned from an advanced alien race.

The Alliance joined the Federation and Empire in establishing Aegis in August 3303, but simultaneously pursued its own independent research initiative through the Kaushpoos Pro-Alliance Union at Neville Horizons. When criticised for this apparent contradiction, the Alliance defended its actions by explaining that the Kaushpoos campaign had been planned before Aegis was established and that the resulting instruments would be made available to the joint initiative.

At the Galactic Summit in February 3307, Mahon proposed the Sirius Treaty, which would have expanded Aegis's remit and strengthened international cooperation against the Thargoids. The treaty was left unsigned when the Summit was cancelled following the Neo-Marlinist Liberation Army's Nine Martyrs attack.

### Values and Identity

In a 3301 speech at Alioth University, Mahon articulated the Alliance's core values: "The Alliance of Independent Systems is built upon a single defining principle and that is one of freedom. The freedoms we enjoy range from our personal freedoms as inscribed in the Bill of Human Rights to the freedom of societies and even planets within the Alliance."

He continued: "Both of those organisations [the Federation and Empire] proudly describe their origins as being freedom from the tyranny of others. And yet now they are the tyranny and it is our duty to oppose them, while also ensuring that we don't become them."

This tension — between championing independence and avoiding the authoritarian tendencies of its rivals — remains central to Alliance identity. Whether the Alliance can maintain this balance while facing existential threats from the Thargoids and internal pressure from corporate partners like Sirius Corporation is one of the great unanswered questions of 34th-century politics.
