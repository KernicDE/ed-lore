## Biography

### Early History and the Caker Rebellion

Alioth's history is defined by resistance. Colonised in the early years of interstellar expansion, the system became a centre of the hydrogen fuel trade by 2530. The Old Worlds — including Alioth — raised fuel prices to the Federation and Empire while selling cheaply to non-aligned systems, making Alioth economically significant but politically vulnerable.

By the mid-26th century, the moon Fruitcake had become a flashpoint. Its inhabitants chafed under the increasingly harsh control of an Imperial Governor. In 2541, a resistance movement calling itself the "Cakers" emerged, launching terrorist attacks against the Imperial regime. Their operations grew in audacity: in 2559, they attempted a suicide hijacking of the Imperial tourist liner *Everichon*; in 2583, they downed an unmarked Federal dropship carrying mercenaries tasked with capturing a major fuel refinery.

The Federation attempted to exploit the chaos. In 2600, President Sadiq Kessler voided Alioth's remaining Federal membership requirements and offered the system full membership, hoping to galvanise pro-Federation sentiment. Instead, the offer divided the Cakers into rival pro-Federation and pro-independence camps. The pro-independence faction prevailed in 2603 under Quentin Devises, who secured control of Fruitcake and declared it a free trade port. After stringing along Federal diplomats for three years, Devises formally rejected the Federation's offer in 2606.

The Empire and Federation would not tolerate defiance indefinitely. In 2617, the execution of munitions worker Tom Quaterson for selling food coupons to the poor triggered a general uprising. The Governor's residence on Fruitcake was stormed; workers seized gas platforms and demanded independence. In an unprecedented move, the Federation and Empire cooperated to retake the system in 2618. Devises was captured and executed. The superpowers divided Alioth's assets: Fruitcake became a Federal Protectorate renamed Gordonworld after Federal General Charles C. Gordon, while all local space facilities were conceded to Imperial rule.

### The 3228 Revolt and the Birth of the Alliance

For centuries, Alioth remained partitioned. The turning point came in 3228, when Federal corporations supplying New California — one of Alioth's main population centres — attempted to raise prices. This triggered a full-scale revolt. Citizens took over ships berthed in the space station and rode out to assault corporate workers at the gas mining stations.

Federal and Imperial forces arrived within days, but fighters from Alioth and neighbouring independent systems joined forces to repel both superpowers. Mic Turner, a pilot born in 3205 who had moved to New California just before the rebellion, was among the first to fly out to the orbital platforms. His skill and courage won him respect among his peers. It was here he met Meredith Argent, a scientist who led one of the Caker cells on the gas platforms and was a direct descendant of Walter Argent, a wealthy 27th-century industrialist from Ayethi.

The Empire withdrew first, unwilling to commit to a protracted campaign far from its centre of power. The Federation followed when it realised how unpopular the war had become among its own citizens. In the wake of victory, Turner and Argent proposed a new political unit for those who wished to exist outside both superpowers. In 3230, the Alliance of Independent Systems was formally established on Alioth. Its founding principle was simple: to protect and support independent systems while preserving their autonomy. Over the next two decades, more than twenty systems joined.

Argent and Turner remained partners for life. In 3252, Turner disappeared while testing the specially designed exploration ship *Turner's Quest* in the Pleiades, searching for the Thargoids. Argent later launched a second ship, *Argent's Quest*, which reportedly discovered a Thargoid outpost and returned to Alioth with a captured Thargoid vessel. According to leaked documents, INRA contacted the pilot, and the Thargoid ship left Alioth the same day — destination unknown.

### Modern Alioth and Alliance Governance

Today, Alioth is the political and economic heart of the Alliance. The system hosts the Alliance Assembly, where councillors from member systems establish common policies and treaties. The Prime Minister — currently Edmund Mahon, who has held office since 3301 — maintains his residence here. The Council of Admirals, which commands the Alliance Defence Force, also operates from Alioth.

The system is permit-locked: pilots require Alliance authorization to enter, reflecting its status as the superpower's capital. With a population of nearly 15 billion and a High Tech economy supported by a secondary Service sector, Alioth is one of the most developed systems in the galaxy. Security is high, and the controlling faction is the Alioth Independents.

### Corporate and Industrial Presence

Alioth has long been a centre of shipbuilding and advanced technology. In February 3307, Lakon Spaceways moved its administrative headquarters to the Lakon Spaceways Central planetary port on Alioth 1 a, following the Alliance's purchase of a majority sharehold in the company. The move was designed to retain Lakon as a key defence contractor for the Alliance Defence Force after an attempted hostile takeover by Core Dynamics was thwarted. All shipyards within the Alioth system offer a permanent 5% discount on Lakon vessels.

In January 3308, Sirius Corporation established a permanent presence in Alioth as part of its strategic defence pact with the Alliance. Sirius Gov, the megacorp's governmental division, now maintains advisors within Alliance Defence Force and government departments. The pact has proved controversial: Councillor Nakato Kaine of Tionisla has argued that the presence of Sirius Gov is illegal because it was not ratified by the Assembly, and that classification of the matter as military has blocked civil action to reverse it.

### Key Events in Recent History

**The Lugh Crisis (3301):** When Éamonn Uí Laoghire, spokesperson for the Crimson State Group's independence bid, arrived in Alioth seeking Alliance recognition for Lugh, Prime Minister Mahon refused. Addressing the Alliance Assembly, he stated: "It is not the place of the Alliance to intervene in matters of legitimate Federal governance. No laws have been broken, no rights have been abused." The decision demonstrated Alioth's commitment to diplomatic neutrality even when ideological sympathies might suggest otherwise.

**The Kincaid Conspiracy (3305):** President Gibson Kincaid attempted to move the Alliance's political centre from Alioth to Zaonce, intending to establish himself as supreme ruler. The conspiracy was exposed, Kincaid was removed from office and charged with treason, and the Assembly abolished the presidency entirely in June 3305.

**The Nine Martyrs (3307):** Alioth was among the starports hit by the NMLA's coordinated bombing campaign on 11 March 3307. The attack targeted civilians across all three superpowers and led to the cancellation of the Galactic Summit.

**The Reorte Conflict (3308):** When Sirius Corporation attempted to expand its influence into the Reorte system — part of the Lave Cluster and historically allied with Alioth — conflict erupted. The Alliance ultimately retained Reorte, but the incident exposed tensions between pro-Sirius and pro-independence factions within Alliance space.

### Significance

Alioth represents something rare in the 34th century: a system that defeated both the Federation and the Empire not once, but twice. From the Caker rebellions of the 26th century to the 3228 revolt that birthed a superpower, Alioth has consistently resisted external domination. Its history is written in the names of its worlds — Gordonworld, Fruitcake, New California — each reflecting a different chapter of occupation, rebellion, and independence.

As the Alliance faces new pressures from corporate partners like Sirius Corporation and existential threats from the Thargoids, Alioth remains the symbolic and administrative anchor of the third superpower. The question that has defined the system for a thousand years — whether independence can be maintained in the face of overwhelming power — remains unanswered.
