## Biography

Capitol is the capital world of the Empire, a sprawling urban planet that serves as the political, cultural, and military heart of Imperial civilisation. Located on Achenar 6d, the city of Capitol was founded by Marlin Duval's colonising fleet and later transformed by her brother Henson into the seat of Imperial power. The planet's surface is dominated by the Imperial Palace — a vast structure of pink glass-like material that shimmers in the sunlight and glows as it re-emits energy at night.

The Palace complex contains the Emperor's private residences, the Imperial Senate chamber, the Hall of Martyrs (where state funerals for Imperial royalty are held), and numerous administrative offices. The Imperial Navy Medical Facility, located near the Palace, has treated generations of Emperors and their families; Emperor Hengist Duval died en route to this facility after his assassination in 3301. The city extends outward from the Palace in concentric rings, with nobles and high-ranking officials residing in the inner districts and lower social classes in the outer rings — a physical manifestation of the Empire's hierarchical structure.

Capitol has been the stage for many of the Empire's most dramatic moments. In August 3301, millions gathered to celebrate Emperor Hengist Duval's wedding to Florence Lavigny, only to witness his assassination by Brendan Paul Darius. The subsequent state funeral in September 3301 drew dignitaries from across the galaxy. In October 3301, Arissa Lavigny-Duval's coronation procession wound through streets packed with Imperial citizens. Security is always heavy, but it has been drastically increased during crises — most notably during the Emperor's Dawn insurgency, when threats against the new Emperor prompted deployment of additional Imperial Naval Infantry.

Foreign visitors are welcomed at the Palace by staff who use subtle pro-Imperial propaganda, but outside the Palace grounds, Federation accents are met with scorn unless money can be made from the visitor. The city's population of over 16 billion makes it one of the most densely populated worlds in the Empire, and its economy — focused on refinery and service industries — supports the vast Imperial bureaucracy that governs the superpower's territory.
