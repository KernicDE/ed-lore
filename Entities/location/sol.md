## Biography

Sol is the G2-V main sequence star around which human civilisation originated approximately 4.5 billion years after the system's formation. Every major galactic power traces its ultimate origins to this single star system, and every coordinate in human space is measured relative to its position — making Sol the literal centre of the galactic map.

Human expansion from Sol began tentatively. Permanent Moon bases were established in the 2070s, and the first human landed on Mars in the same decade. The 2080s saw the construction of the first starports, interstellar probes launched toward Alpha Centauri and Barnard's Star, and heavy industrialisation of the Moon. In 2100, two pivotal discoveries reshaped humanity's future: ancient fossils were found on Mars, and the existence of hyperspace was confirmed. Frame Shift Drive technology followed, and by the 2130s hyperspace-capable probes had been dispatched to all nearby star systems.

The Federation was formally established in 2242, unifying Earth's remaining nations and off-world colonies. Mars was successfully terraformed in 2291, becoming the first planet transformed into an Earth-like world. However, Sol's absolute dominance ended in 2325, when the Federation suffered a devastating defeat at the Battle of Achenar against the newly formed Empire. To appease member systems dissatisfied with Earth's leadership, the Federal capital was transferred to Mars, though Earth remained the symbolic heart of the Federation.

By the 34th century, Sol's political importance has diminished relative to rising systems like Achenar, Alioth, and Cubeo. Yet it remains one of the most heavily populated systems in human space, with over 18 billion inhabitants across its worlds and orbital habitats. Earth itself has recovered from centuries of ecological damage and is now a protected, lush world and popular tourist destination. Mars hosts the Federal Congress and major government institutions. The system's security is paramount — during the Thargoid conflicts, Sol was heavily fortified, and it has never fallen to external attack.

Sol's cultural significance transcends its political weight. It is the ultimate symbol of human achievement, the reference point for all navigation, and the answer to the question every child in human space learns first: where did we come from?
