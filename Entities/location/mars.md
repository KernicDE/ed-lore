## Biography

Mars holds the distinction of being the first planet successfully terraformed by humanity. The process was completed in 2291, transforming the red planet into an Earth-like world with a breathable atmosphere and viable ecosystem. An earlier experimental attempt in the 2170s had been abandoned, but the second effort succeeded and established the template for subsequent terraforming projects across human space.

The planet's political significance increased dramatically after the Battle of Achenar in 2325. The Federation's defeat and the subsequent independence of the Empire created deep discontent among Federal member systems, many of whom blamed Earth's leadership for the disaster. To stabilise the Federation, the capital was relocated from Earth to Mars — a move that symbolised a fresh start while maintaining Sol's central position in Federal space. The Federal Congress now meets on Mars, alongside major government institutions and corporate headquarters.

Mars has been the backdrop for numerous pivotal political moments. In 3301, Shadow President Zachary Hudson held a press conference on Mars announcing his intention to seek Congressional support for a Vote of No Confidence against President Jasmina Halsey. The successful vote, held shortly after Halsey's disappearance aboard Starship One, installed Hudson as President and shifted Federal politics sharply toward Republican militarism. Mars has also hosted Federal Navy facilities, corporate summits, and diplomatic receptions.

As a terraformed world, Mars represents both the triumph and the fragility of human environmental engineering. Its ecosystem requires ongoing maintenance, and its population of billions depends on imported resources from across Federal space. The planet's economy is diversified, with significant refinery, service, and high-tech sectors. Despite the shift of galactic commerce to systems like Alioth and Cubeo, Mars remains the administrative centre of the largest superpower in human space — a position it has held for nearly a millennium.
