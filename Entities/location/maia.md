## Biography

Maia is a system in the Pleiades Nebula, roughly 380 light years from Sol. Prior to 3301 it was a remote, largely uninhabited system on the fringes of the inhabited bubble. That changed with the emergence of the Unknown Artefact phenomenon.

### The Unknown Artefact Crisis (3301)

The system first entered galactic headlines on 10 July 3301, when Dr Arcanonn of the Canonn Interstellar Research Group announced a breakthrough: Unknown Artefacts (UAs) were found to emit a distorted Morse code signal broadcasting the identity of the nearest celestial body. This discovery, made in part through research conducted in Maia, ignited scientific interest in the Pleiades region.

By December 3301, the UA "technological plague" — a mysterious malfunction affecting station systems — had spread to Obsidian Orbital in Maia. The station, which had been constructed earlier that year through a community-driven campaign, suffered service suspensions, a bitter disappointment to the pilots who had helped build it.

### Barnacles and Meta-Alloys (3302)

On 14 January 3302, a fragmentary, unidentified signal was received at Obsidian Orbital. Although a nearby black hole prevented precise vector determination, the degraded transmission contained coordinates that led pilots to barnacle sites — alien structures producing a material dubbed "meta-alloys." The Canonn confirmed barnacle discoveries on multiple planets in the Pleiades, with the Maia signal playing a direct role in the search.

Professor Ishmael Palin, formerly of the Palin Institute, established a research base at Obsidian Orbital and issued a community appeal for meta-alloy deliveries, hoping to determine whether the material could counter the technological plague. The response was enthusiastic, but the campaign was marred by anti-meta-alloy protests that turned violent on 29 January 3302, with protestors blockading the station and attacking delivery ships.

Palin's research was abruptly halted when he and his team disappeared en route to Maia B1BA in February 3302. Adding to the mystery, thousands of tonnes of meta-alloys delivered for his research were reportedly sold on the black market to front companies for an unknown conglomerate. Dr Arcanonn of the Canonn received a credible death threat warning him against visiting Palin in Maia, and an investigation identified a research assistant as a leak.

### Superpower Rivalry and Blockade (3302)

Despite these setbacks, Maia's strategic importance grew. The Federation and Empire both used the system as a logistics hub for constructing outposts deeper in the Pleiades, with supply campaigns running through Obsidian Orbital. In October 3302, following Palin's announcement of a breakthrough in meta-alloy defensive applications, the Federation deployed a Farragut-class Battlecruiser to blockade Maia A 3 a near Palin's personal research base. The move drew condemnation from Alliance Prime Minister Edmund Mahon and Emperor Arissa Lavigny-Duval.

Simultaneously, the Ant Hill Mob — Maia's controlling faction — launched an offensive against the Pleiades Resource Enterprise, a Federation-aligned corporation that had entered the system. The conflict escalated into a week-long proxy war between Imperial and Federal pilots, with the Ant Hill Mob ultimately prevailing in November 3302.

### Thargoid Escalation (3303)

Maia became a flashpoint in the emerging Thargoid conflict. In September 3303, the Federation confirmed that a Farragut-class Battlecruiser and accompanying vessels had been destroyed by Thargoid Interceptor-class vessels in the Maia system — the first confirmed destruction of a Federal capital ship by Thargoid forces. President Zachary Hudson declared the Thargoids an enemy of the Federation and granted Admiral Aden Tanner total authority and full Federal Navy resources to address the threat.

### Later Developments

Following the Thargoid escalation, Maia remained a key system in the Pleiades conflict zone. Aegis, the tri-superpower anti-xeno initiative, conducted operations in the region. The system continued to serve as a waypoint for pilots involved in anti-Thargoid combat and research, with Obsidian Orbital remaining a vital port of call in the nebula.

By the late 3300s, Maia's population had grown to roughly 139,000 and its economy shifted toward industrial and extraction activities, reflecting its role as a frontier outpost on the boundary between human and Thargoid space.
