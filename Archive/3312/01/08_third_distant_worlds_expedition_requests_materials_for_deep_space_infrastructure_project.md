The Distant Worlds working group has issued a call for mined and manufactured materials to support the construction of a new deep-space facility, intended to aid future exploration of remote galactic regions far beyond established survey routes.
Hermine Delarue, Executive Operative for Deep Space Surveys, stated:
“DSS has longstanding experience in supporting large-scale exploration efforts, most notably the construction of Explorers’ Anchorage during the Distant Worlds II initiative in 3305.”
“This new project focuses on preparing a remote site for long-range scientific operations. We aim to establish essential infrastructure for survey crews venturing into seldom-charted regions. Independent pilots are invited to help lay the foundations for the next era of deep-space discovery.”
Timotheus Haddard, quartermaster for an affiliated Distant Worlds support carrier, added:
“Initial requirements centre on core construction needs. We’re currently prioritising bromellite, power generators and thermal cooling units to begin establishing the primary framework.”
All interested parties have been invited to head to Alrai and register at Surveyor’s Reach before delivering the required commodities. DSS are reportedly offering a class 5 pre-engineered cargo rack to project contributors.
The expedition itself begins on the 18th of January, and organisers have issued an open invitation to any Commanders that wish to join the adventure.
